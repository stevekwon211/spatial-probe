mini-PRISM scene representation memo nuScenes · scene-0061 2026-07-06

Stop projecting texture at render time. Bake it, and mark what no camera saw.

The grey ground is not a shader-tuning bug — it is two structural failures of forward-projecting a road from cameras that sit at ego height. The fix is not to cover the grey. It is to texture exactly as far as a camera actually looked, and render the rest as an honest gap. Below: why the current pipeline is capped, the full option matrix, and a two-horizon pipeline you can ship this week.

Honesty tier model for the viewer

Tier 0 · measured

Honest but coarse

The Occ3D three-state grid, rendered as three states. Occupied / free / unknown. The layer a reviewer reads a call from. Nothing above may repaint its UNKNOWN as a surface.

Tier 1 · inferred

Legible but derived

Baked texture + tracked dynamics, layered over Tier 0 as an aid. Camera-verified pixels read crisp; grazing-fill or distant-view pixels read visibly softened.

Tier 2 · speculative

Photoreal but invented

The Gaussian splat. Off by default, badged SPECULATIVE. Answers "read the sign / see the car." Never the layer a QA decision is read from.

01

Why the current approach is structurally capped

Two independent limits, one per goal. Neither is a parameter you can turn up. The geometry is data-resolution-bound; the texture failure is a projection-model error. Naming them correctly is what tells you the fix belongs in the prep step, not the fragment shader.

Geometry — a data problem wearing a meshing costume

The 0.4 m Occ3D voxel + Manifold Dual Contouring mesh is coarse and torn not because the mesher is weak, but because the input grid is coarse and the occupancy is sparse. A better isosurface over the same grid buys almost nothing. The leverage is in changing the geometry source, not the contouring algorithm. And the coarseness is data-truth: 0.4 m is the GT resolution. For the QA layer that is a feature — surfacing it honestly is the point — so this layer stays. The fidelity upgrade belongs to a separate layer.

Root cause · coverage

No camera looks down

All six nuScenes cameras sit at ego height (~1.5 m). The road within a few metres of the ego is either below the FOV or seen at a near-horizontal ray. The live shader binds uTex0..5 — frame-0's six cameras only — so it can only ever paint what frame-0 saw. That is a hard coverage hole no per-fragment weighting can fill.

In code: projective.ts:38 declares exactly 6 samplers; :92-94 pads to 6 and never binds another keyframe.

Root cause · geometry

Grazing means stretch

For a flat plane the incidence n·dir is ~0 everywhere far from the ego, so one image column smears across a long strip of road — streaks at a low threshold. The code's own grazing gate then culls exactly that road and drops to the grey fallback. The gate that fixes obstacle smear is what greys the ground.

In code: projective.ts:53 if (f <= 0.15) return vec4(0.0); data.ts:97 then paints the tile neutral grey 0.5.

The unexploited signal — already half-built in the repo

The ego drives 10–20 m. Every road patch that grazes frame-0 was driven straight over — seen near-nadir, up close — by some other keyframe. The prep already exploits this for obstacles: voxel_colors_multiframe projects all 39 keyframes into frame-0 ego. But it is baked at the 0.4 m voxel resolution (can't read a lane line), used only as a fallback, and the ground gets none of it.

the fix is not a new signal  →  it is running the projection the repo already has, into a high-res per-texel buffer, offline, with best-view selection instead of nearest-z.

The honesty axis is the real discriminator

Watertight methods — Poisson, closed SDF, splat-to-mesh — hallucinate surface across unobserved gaps (Screened Poisson's documented "bulging artifact where data are missing"). That is exactly the confident-wrong-surface the QA layer must never present. Occupancy voxels are the one representation that natively carries FREE / OCCUPIED / UNKNOWN. So the honest layer stays occupancy-based, and — the key measured fact — Occ3D already ships a per-voxel camera-visibility mask (arXiv 2304.14365): for each occupied voxel, was it hit by any camera ray? The pipeline currently ignores it and decides "seen" from a render-time grazing heuristic instead. That is the root structural gap.

03

The trust-tier stack — what renders, and what may never overwrite what

The two product goals conflict, so one representation cannot serve both. Split the scene into three purpose-built layers stacked in trust order. A decoration above can never repaint the measured layer's UNKNOWN as a surface.

04

Recommended pipeline — two horizons, ranked

Do not replace the occupancy mesh, and do not fix this in the live shader. The through-line: stop forward-projecting appearance at render time; bake it once, offline, into a resolution-independent per-texel buffer with explicit best-view selection and a per-texel coverage channel. That is what makes texture both complete (uses all 39 frames' near-nadir views) and honest (verified-vs-unseen is a stored, renderable fact, not a guess).

Horizon A

Near-term — lands in this web pipeline, days, $0 GPU

1

IPM ground orthophoto fixes the confirmed grey ground

Reuse the existing 39-keyframe loop in prep_occ.py. For each keyframe's 6 cams, inverse-project the road pixels to world (x,y) via a per-cell ground homography — use the existing ground_heightfield z per column, not one global plane, so curbs don't smear. Accumulate into a world-aligned BEV raster at ~5 cm/texel. Per texel keep argmax( head-on × near × static × unoccluded ): head-on rewards the frame that drove most-nadir over the patch, static = 0 inside a nuScenes 3D box (kills ghost cars), unoccluded from the existing depth prepass. Emit one orthophoto PNG + transform; replace the grey ground color in data.ts:97 with a UV sample. Where coverage == 0, render the honest gap style — never a fabricated color.

2

Camera-visibility gate replaces the grazing heuristic the root-cause honesty fix

Export Occ3D's per-voxel camera-visibility mask into the web payload as a measured bit. Drive the seen/unseen decision from that mask, not the render-time grazing gate: a voxel observed by any keyframe is eligible for crisp texture (pull the best head-on keyframe — this is the legitimate labeling-pinhole fill); a voxel unobserved in every keyframe (the blind spot under the ego) renders INFERRED — desaturated, blurred, hatched — or as a gap. Keep the grazing gate only as a per-view blend weight. This turns "seen" from a heuristic into a labelled fact.

impact high feasibility high honesty highest cost data plumbing, no GPU
3

Dynamic-object layer from box tracks un-ghost the moving actors

Pull moving cars/peds from the nuScenes 3D box tracks into their own time-stamped layer, marked TRACKED. Mask their multi-sweep ghost tail out of the static layers (voxels inside a moving box over time). A smeared car shown as solid static geometry is a fabricated obstacle — this removes one of the three named fabrication classes.

Horizon B

Longer-term — the representation to move to for "really proper"

5

MVS-Texturing UV atlas for the obstacle mesh "Let There Be Color", Waechter 2014

The general form of the orthophoto for non-planar geometry. xatlas-unwrap the DC mesh; a best-view MRF picks the single best of all 39×6 images per face (data = projected area × sharpness, gated by occlusion + photo-consistency outlier reject; Potts smoothness via graph cut), then global seam-leveling + Poisson blend hides exposure seams. Bake to one atlas PNG sampled with static UVs. Sequence after A and after mesh de-tearing — atlas quality is capped by the shredded 0.4 m mesh (12% of voxels ≤1 neighbour); texturing a torn mesh just makes torn texture.

6

Multi-sweep LiDAR TSDF for a finer obstacle mesh optional geometry upgrade

Accumulate all 39 keyframe LiDAR sweeps into an offline TSDF (VDBFusion on CPU or nvblox), removing dynamic points first via the box tracks. Ship the fused mesh as a static glTF. Accumulation beats the 32-beam sparsity on static structure and gives finer-than-0.4 m near-range surface with an honest free/unknown truncation. A better mesh for Tier 1 to texture.

impact medfeasibility medhonesty TSDF free/unknowncost CPU offline, $0 serve
7

North star — LiDAR-augmented GS+SDF hybrid GS-SDF / 2DGS-TSDF lineage

One optimization yielding both a clean metric surface (LiDAR-anchored) and photoreal texture. This is the cleanest textured surface on a LiDAR budget if it matures on surround-view nuScenes — today it is research-early on driving + sparse 32-beam. Hard requirement before it can ever approach the QA tier: intersect the extracted surface with the observed/occupancy mask so watertightness never hallucinates confirmed surface over unknown space, plus a per-Gaussian uncertainty channel. Until then the splat stays quarantined as the badged Tier-2 appearance aid.

One line to enforce everywhere — the legitimate-fill rule

Fill or interpolate texture only into camera-observed voxels. A voxel unobserved in every keyframe is never given a confident surface. The far road that grazes frame-0 but was driven over 10 keyframes later is a legitimate fill; the down-looking blind spot under the ego is the canonical lie.

legitimate fill  =  observed-elsewhere  ·  lie  =  observed-nowhere  ·  the visibility mask IS the discriminator

Key prior art

  • Occ3D arXiv 2304.14365 — three-state grid + per-voxel LiDAR & camera visibility masks; eval on observed voxels only. The data-native measured bit.
  • Let There Be Color! Waechter et al., ECCV 2014 — best-view MRF (projected area/gradient, Potts smoothness) + seam-leveling + Poisson blend. Ref impl nmoehrle/mvs-texturing.
  • Street Gaussians arXiv 2401.01339, ECCV 2024 — static/dynamic decomposition, per-actor rigid groups + 4D SH. DrivingGaussian CVPR 2024 — nuScenes surround-native.
  • StreetSurf arXiv 2306.04988 — hash-grid SDF, mono-depth priors, road-surface init, 1–2 h on a single RTX-3090; no dynamic decomposition.
  • IPM / BEV homography — plane→plane road resampling, flat-road assumption + its incline failure. RoadBEV arXiv 2404.06605 — learned road elevation (stereo 0.5 cm) to relax flatness.
  • 2DGS arXiv 2403.17888 · GS-SDF arXiv 2503.10170 · RaDe-GS 2406.01467 — LiDAR-augmented splat+SDF meshing; research-early on driving.
  • Screened Poisson Kazhdan/Hoppe, ACM TOG 2013 — watertight, "bulging artifact where data are missing". The honesty-hostile baseline.
  • COLMAP / MP-SfM arXiv 2504.20040 — "fails catastrophically for triplets with least overlap"; assumes sufficient overlap + texture. Why camera-only MVS is rejected.
  • Uncertainty viz / cartography — transparency, value, crispness effective; hatching the standard extrinsic overlay; hue/saturation poor. Basis for the honesty encoding.
  • NeRO arXiv 2405.10554 — neural road orthophoto MLP (x,y)→(z,RGB); the coverage-gated fallback if the pure IPM mosaic reads too blurry.
