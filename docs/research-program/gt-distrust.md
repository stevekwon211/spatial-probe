Program order position #5 (occquery -> dynfield -> value-of-correction -> asof -> gt-distrust -> vis-calibration). NOTE: the orchestrator labeled this "Stage 3"; in the canonical spatial-probe program (PLAN.md:30-42, anchors:259) gt-distrust is the 5th experiment. This plan is the 5th-program / gt-distrust paper plan regardless of the orchestrator's "Stage 3" label. STATUS: PROVISIONAL, no experiment code exists yet (only the shared seed primitive occlusion_depth() in src/probe/raycast.py:91-102, tested but unused). Occ3D-nuScenes mini IS present locally (10 scenes; semantics + mask_lidar + mask_camera per labels.npz, all (200,200,16)); per-frame mask_lidar observes ~22% of voxels, mask_camera ~20%, the two masks disagree on ~2.3% — verified 2026-06-21.

## Motivation
THE GAP. Camera-only 3D occupancy is trained and benchmarked against "dense" ground truth (Occ3D-nuScenes, SurroundOcc, OpenOccupancy) that is MANUFACTURED, not observed: dense GT = multi-sweep LiDAR accumulation + surface reconstruction (TSDF/VDBFusion/Poisson) to fill holes. The accumulation labels voxels the sensor never actually saw at any single instant — it labels THROUGH occluders. On the local Occ3D mini, the dense semantics field calls ~93% of voxels free/occupied, but a single LiDAR sweep observes only ~22% of voxels (verified 2026-06-21); the other ~71% are "labeled-but-unobserved." Occ3D ships a binary per-voxel visibility mask (mask_lidar / mask_camera), and the field's standard protocols (SparseOcc, RayIoU) already condition on it — but a BINARY observed/unobserved flag is a coarse instrument: it says a voxel is unseen, not HOW BADLY occluded it is. Two unobserved voxels — one just past a thin railing, one buried behind a parked truck two cars deep — get the identical "untrusted" stamp, yet the reconstruction error on the deep one is far larger. THE PRECISE CLAIM: a CONTINUOUS per-voxel occlusion-DEPTH score (how many occupied cells a ray from the ego must pierce to reach the voxel — exactly src/probe/raycast.py:occlusion_depth) carries information ABOVE the binary mask about which labels are untrustworthy. WHY IT MATTERS: if true, the score is a cheap, deterministic GT-audit tool — it ranks which voxels of a "dense" benchmark to down-weight in training/eval, and it explains a slice of camera-only occupancy error as label noise rather than model failure. WHY NOW / WHY CHEAP HERE: the load-bearing operation is the DDA ray-march already built, tested, and dead-but-seeded for exactly this (raycast.py:94 comment: "Used later by gt-distrust"); the Occ3D adapter already exposes the masked/dense views (occ3d.py:43-64, load_scene mask= param); the data is local; everything runs on CPU. This is the field's strongest move-class applied honestly: reform the MEASUREMENT of label trust (like RayIoU reformed surface scoring) rather than post an accuracy number. It is also the natural payoff of the occquery substrate — occquery deliberately does NOT apply the per-frame mask to its oracle (occ3d.py:51-56; PLAN.md:170-177); the masked observed view it set aside IS this paper's conditioning variable.

## Central hypothesis
A continuous per-voxel occlusion-depth score d(v) (the number of occupied cells a ray from the ego sensor origin pierces before reaching voxel v, computed by DDA ray-march over the occupancy field) predicts which Occ3D-nuScenes occupancy GT labels are untrustworthy — labeled through an occluder — with information STRICTLY BEYOND the binary released LiDAR-visibility mask and BEYOND a distance-from-ego baseline. Operationally falsifiable by ONE head-to-head: on GT with synthetically-injected known label errors whose injection probability rises with true occlusion, d(v) detects the injected errors with AP strictly above (a) an occlusion-depth-shuffled control and (b) a distance-from-ego ranker AND (c) the binary visibility mask used as a flat ranker; and excluding the top-d(v) voxels degrades a released occupancy checkpoint's RayIoU strictly more than excluding a range/class/visibility-matched control mask of equal size. The relative gaps (d vs each control) are load-bearing; no absolute AP threshold is.

## Sub-hypotheses
- H1 (existence / mechanism, the headline; PARTLY oracle-free): occlusion DEPTH is not redundant with the binary visibility mask. Construct it as a non-identifiability witness in the occquery style: among voxels the LiDAR mask marks IDENTICALLY unobserved (mask_lidar==0), the dense GT reconstruction error is monotone-increasing in d(v). Strongest oracle-free form: two voxels with identical (mask value, semantic class, range-from-ego) but different d(v) — the binary mask MUST stamp them identically, d(v) distinguishes them. Where reconstruction error needs an oracle, it is the synthetic-injection oracle of H2, not an external GT. Kill: error is flat in d(v) within the unobserved stratum (depth adds nothing over the binary mask).
- H2 (detection power, self-controlled oracle): on GT corrupted by synthetic-occluder error injection where P(corrupt voxel v) increases with the TRUE occlusion depth of v, ranking voxels by d(v) recovers the injected errors with AP strictly greater than (a) a d-shuffled control (same score distribution, destroyed voxel correspondence), (b) a distance-from-ego ranker, and (c) the binary mask as a flat ranker. Reported as a full precision-recall / AP-vs-injection-rate curve, not one cutoff. Kill: AP indistinguishable from the d-shuffled control (at chance) OR not above the distance baseline.
- H3 (downstream consequence, external oracle): excluding the voxels d(v) flags as untrustworthy from a RELEASED camera-only occupancy checkpoint's evaluation changes its mIoU AND RayIoU strictly MORE (per voxel removed) than excluding a control mask matched on range, semantic-class, and binary-visibility. I.e. the flagged labels are where the checkpoint and the GT disagree most — evidence the flags mark genuinely low-trust GT, not arbitrary voxels. Reported as an exclusion-budget vs metric-change curve. Kill: the downstream delta is statistically indistinguishable from the matched control across the budget curve.
- H4 (boundary / scope discipline, deductive): there exists a derivable occlusion-depth regime where the score CANNOT help — at d(v)=0 (directly visible) the label is sensor-supported and trust is high by construction; at very large d(v) ALL labels are pure reconstruction guesswork and the score saturates (no ranking signal left). The useful, falsifiable band is intermediate d(v). State this as a predicted, pre-registered shape of the AP-vs-d curve (rises then saturates), derived from the geometry, not discovered post-hoc.

## Success criteria (pre-registered)
- TIER-SMOKE (instrument works, NOT a scientific result): occlusion_depth() ray-marches from the ego origin to every voxel center over the Occ3D mini dense occupancy and produces a per-voxel d(v) field; on the 10 local mini scenes d(v) agrees with the LiDAR visibility mask in the expected direction (high-d voxels are predominantly mask_lidar==0) — checked against mask_LIDAR, never mask_camera (the camera mask shares the flag's own forward ray-cast and is near-tautological; anchors:216-218). Runs on CPU. >= N new unit tests green (DDA-from-ego correctness on known geometry, monotonicity of d along a ray, injection harness reproducibility). Pre-registered as smoke only.
- TIER-PUBLISHABLE-A (H1+H2, the load-bearing result, oracle = self-controlled injection): on Occ3D-nuScenes val (NOT mini — mini has only 10 scenes and is for instrument-hardening), with the synthetic-occluder error-injection oracle, injected-error detection AP by d(v) beats ALL THREE controls (d-shuffled, distance-from-ego, binary-mask-flat) by a pre-registered RELATIVE margin reported as a full AP-vs-injection-rate curve with per-scene bootstrap 95% CIs. The relative gap to the d-shuffled control is the single load-bearing number. The injection oracle's construction code + held-out scene IDs are released for external recompute (anchors:25-27).
- TIER-PUBLISHABLE-B (H3, external downstream oracle): excluding top-d(v) voxels from >= 1 released camera-only occupancy checkpoint's evaluation moves mIoU AND RayIoU strictly more than a range/class/visibility-matched control mask of equal voxel count, across a pre-registered exclusion-budget curve, with CIs that separate the two curves. Both arms (d-flagged and matched-control) are run on the IDENTICAL checkpoint and protocol so the only varied factor is which voxels are excluded.
- TIER-STRONG (adoption, explicitly a stretch): the continuous per-voxel trust score + the injection protocol + the matched-control construction are released as a reusable GT-audit tool that runs on any Occ3D-format field, with the reproduction harness, such that a third party can audit a NEW occupancy dataset's dense-GT trust profile. No leaderboard exists for this; 'strong' = adoptable, not a SOTA number.
- META: every self-chosen cutoff (the d-threshold defining 'untrustworthy', the injection rate, the exclusion budget, AP operating point) is pre-registered AND reported as a full continuous curve; the RELATIVE gaps (d vs each control) are the claims, never an absolute AP/mIoU figure (anchors:28-31). A fixed evaluation budget — number of scenes, number of injected voxels per scene, number of seeds (>= 3), number of checkpoints — is declared BEFORE the val run.

## Kill / pivot criteria (pre-registered)
- KILL-1 (H2 chance): injected-error detection AP by d(v) is statistically indistinguishable from the d-shuffled control across the injection-rate curve (CIs overlap everywhere). Then occlusion depth carries no error-prediction signal beyond noise — the central claim is FALSE. This is a clean, honest negative and is reported as the headline, not buried (anchors:37; frontier rule 2).
- KILL-2 (depth redundant with the binary mask): d(v) beats the distance baseline but does NOT beat the binary visibility mask used as a flat ranker — i.e. all the signal is 'observed vs unobserved', none is in the DEPTH gradient. Then the paper's specific contribution (continuous depth > binary flag) collapses to the already-published binary mask. PIVOT: demote to a calibrated descriptive study of WHERE the binary mask is insufficient (the ~2.3% mask_lidar != mask_camera disagreement region measured locally), or fold the finding into vis-calibration; do not claim depth as the contribution.
- KILL-3 (H3 indistinguishable): excluding d-flagged voxels moves mIoU/RayIoU no more than the matched control across the whole budget curve. Then the flags do not mark labels the model actually fails on — they are not 'untrustworthy' in any downstream sense. PIVOT: keep H1+H2 as a GT-geometry result only and explicitly drop the downstream claim, OR re-examine whether the released checkpoint was itself trained with the mask (confound — its errors already avoid unobserved voxels).
- KILL-4 (circular-oracle contamination detected): if agreement/AP is high ONLY against mask_camera (which shares the flag's own forward ray-cast) and vanishes against mask_lidar, the result is an artifact of measuring the score against its own construction. The camera-mask leg is dropped a priori for this reason (anchors:223-225); if a reviewer reconstructs a camera-mask result and it disagrees with the lidar-mask result, treat the lidar-mask result as authoritative and the camera one as tautological.
- KILL-5 (injection oracle is unfaithful): if the synthetic injection's error distribution does not resemble real reconstruction error (e.g. real errors are dominated by dynamic-object trails, not occlusion depth), the self-controlled oracle is a wrong proxy and H2 is uninformative about real GT. MITIGATION/CHECK: validate injection faithfulness by comparing the injected-error spatial distribution to the d-stratified disagreement between the dense GT and an independent raw-LiDAR-sweep reconstruction on a held-out scene; if they diverge, state injection-only as a scope limit (frontier rule 3, manufacture-the-oracle honesty).

## Oracle
PRIMARY ORACLE — self-controlled synthetic-occluder error injection (anchors:211-213). You CONTROL the errors so you need not trust any external 'true occupancy'. Procedure: take dense Occ3D GT semantics as the clean reference; inject known label errors (flip/erase/insert occupied) with per-voxel corruption probability P(corrupt v) that INCREASES with the voxel's true occlusion depth d_true(v) — physically motivated since accumulate-then-reconstruct error grows with how occluded a voxel was. The injected-error mask is the exactly-known answer key; d(v) is the predictor. Because the key is injected, H2 needs no external GT (the CAPTURe / Beyond-the-Visible manufacture-the-oracle pattern done honestly). RELEASE injection code + RNG seeds + held-out scene IDs for external recompute. EXTERNAL ORACLE (H3 only) — a released camera-only occupancy CHECKPOINT's predictions vs dense GT, scored by released RayIoU/SparseOcc protocol code (external, re-derivable). AVOIDING THE CIRCULAR ORACLE (central risk; anchors:216-225, h3-findings:6-10): (1) d(v) is a forward ray-cast from the ego and so is the CAMERA mask — 'd agrees with mask_camera' is near-tautological, so the camera-mask leg is DROPPED a priori; the independent reference is mask_LIDAR (different sensor geometry; verified locally to disagree with mask_camera on ~2.3% of voxels), used only as a smoke direction-check, never as the H2 key. (2) H2's key is the INJECTED mask, independent of d(v) by construction. (3) H3's oracle is a model checkpoint's predictions — a different artifact, so downstream evidence cannot be circular with the geometric score. NEVER claim a result against GT sharing the predictor's data source — the occquery H3 'visual-agreement' episode (h3-findings:87-89) is the precedent this plan refuses to repeat.

## Baselines
- B0 BINARY VISIBILITY MASK as flat ranker (the naive baseline that MUST be beaten): rank by released mask_lidar (0=untrusted). Existing practice (Occ3D/SparseOcc already condition on it). If d(v) cannot beat it, the continuous-depth contribution is empty (KILL-2). The gt-distrust analogue of occquery's box-only baseline.
- B1 DISTANCE-FROM-EGO ranker: rank by Euclidean range from ego. Occlusion depth correlates with range, so distance is the obvious confound; d(v) must beat it to show the OCCLUSION structure (rays through occupied cells), not mere farness, predicts untrust (anchors:217; the Waymo influence-over-proximity move).
- B2 d-SHUFFLED control: exact d(v) score distribution with voxel correspondence permuted. Isolates spatial binding of depth-to-voxel from the marginal distribution. AP vs B2 is the load-bearing relative number for H2.
- B3 RANGE/CLASS/VISIBILITY-MATCHED control mask (H3): random equal-size voxel mask stratified to match the d-flagged set over (range bin, semantic class, binary-visibility). Removing it vs the d-flagged set isolates depth from range/class/observed confounds downstream.
- B4 (conditioning control, oracle-free) LiDAR-mask-conditioned: among mask_lidar==0 voxels only, does d(v) still stratify error? Conditions AWAY the binary mask so remaining d-signal is purely the depth gradient — proves the result is not just 'observed vs unobserved'.

## Ablations
- A1 SCORE-FAMILY (isolates the depth mechanism): d(v) = occluder-COUNT (raycast.occlusion_depth as built) vs occluder-PATH-LENGTH (metric distance through occupied cells) vs first-occluder-distance. Each maps to one hypothesis about WHAT in the occlusion makes a label untrustworthy. Report where each helps and where it does not (a null is evidence for the cheaper count score).
- A2 UNKNOWN-POLICY 3-rule sensitivity (reuses the occquery instrument; grid.UnknownPolicy free/occupied/ignored): the ray-march's treatment of UNKNOWN voxels (do they block the ray?) changes d(v). Detection AP must be reported under all three policies; if the H2 result FLIPS across policies, the score is policy-dependent and scope shrinks to the stable regime (the exact occquery falsification pattern, anchors:99-110). This is the geometric-mechanism-isolation ablation done right.
- A3 SENSOR-ORIGIN ablation: ray-march from the LiDAR origin vs each camera origin vs a fused origin. The dense GT is LiDAR-accumulated, so the LiDAR-origin d(v) should be the strongest predictor; a camera-origin d(v) that predicts BETTER would signal the circular-camera-mask contamination (a built-in tripwire for KILL-4).
- A4 INJECTION-MODEL ablation (one factor at a time): vary the injection error TYPE (label-flip vs erase vs insert-phantom-occupied) and the depth-coupling STRENGTH of P(corrupt). Shows the result is not an artifact of one injection recipe; a depth-coupling of ZERO (errors injected uniformly, independent of d) is the critical null — d(v) MUST NOT detect uniformly-injected errors above chance, or the whole benchmark is leaking.
- A5 RANGE-DECOUPLED ablation: within fixed range bins, does d(v) still beat the binary mask? Decouples occlusion depth from distance-from-ego (B1) the way SpatialTunnel decouples vertical-position from depth — confirms the signal is occlusion structure, not range.
- A6 CHECKPOINT ablation (H3): repeat the exclusion study on >= 2 released camera-only checkpoints. If the downstream delta appears on one and not another, report it (and check whether the null-checkpoint was trained WITH the visibility mask, which would already immunize it against unobserved-voxel errors — a confound to surface, not hide).

## Metrics
PRIMARY (relative, load-bearing): for H2, DETECTION AP / area-under-PR of injected-error recovery by d(v) MINUS the same for each control (d-shuffled, distance, binary-mask) — reported as the GAP and as full AP-vs-injection-rate and PR curves with per-scene bootstrap 95% CIs (>= 3 seeds). The gap to the d-shuffled control is THE number. For H3, the per-voxel-removed CHANGE in mIoU and RayIoU when excluding d-flagged voxels MINUS the change when excluding the matched control, as an exclusion-budget curve with CIs separating the two arms. SECONDARY (absolute, internal-validity only, NOT a headline per anchors:28-31): raw detection AP, raw mIoU/RayIoU deltas, Spearman rho between d(v) and per-voxel reconstruction error within the unobserved stratum. RELATIVE-vs-ABSOLUTE FRAMING (the house rule): NO absolute AP or mIoU figure is a result — only the gaps d-vs-control are. Every self-chosen cutoff (d-threshold for 'untrusted', injection rate, exclusion budget, AP operating point) is pre-registered and shown as a continuous curve so a reviewer cannot move a single number to erase the finding. NO cross-protocol comparison: mIoU/RayIoU are computed by ONE released protocol on ONE checkpoint; the same metric name is never compared across modality/checkpoint/split. For H1, the witness is reported as an EXISTENCE/observational separation (binary mask cannot distinguish two voxels d(v) does), plus the Spearman monotonicity within the mask-conditioned stratum — not a score to beat.

## Expected results
EXPECTED (if the hypothesis holds): (H1) within the mask_lidar==0 stratum, dense-GT reconstruction error rises monotonically with d(v) (positive Spearman, CIs excluding 0); the non-identifiability witness holds by construction (two voxels identical in (mask,class,range) but different d). (H2) d(v) detects depth-coupled injected errors with AP a pre-registered relative margin above the d-shuffled control and above distance-from-ego, AND above the binary-mask flat ranker; the AP-vs-d curve RISES then SATURATES (H4's predicted shape) — useful in the intermediate-depth band, flat at d=0 and at extreme d. (H3) excluding top-d(v) voxels moves a released checkpoint's RayIoU more per voxel than the matched control, across the budget curve. FALSIFICATION — EXACTLY what observation kills each claim: (a) if, within the unobserved stratum, error is FLAT in d(v) (Spearman ~0) -> H1 dead, depth adds nothing over the binary mask (KILL-2). (b) if H2 AP overlaps the d-SHUFFLED control's CI at every injection rate -> at chance -> central claim FALSE (KILL-1); this is the single cleanest kill. (c) if H2 AP beats distance but NOT the binary mask -> contribution collapses to the existing binary flag (KILL-2, pivot to descriptive). (d) if uniformly-injected errors (A4, zero depth-coupling) ARE detected above chance -> the benchmark leaks and ALL H2 numbers are void until fixed. (e) if H3 d-flagged exclusion delta overlaps the matched-control CI across the whole budget -> flagged labels are not where the model fails -> downstream claim dropped (KILL-3). (f) if camera-origin d(v) (A3) predicts better than LiDAR-origin d(v) -> suspect circular contamination, re-audit. HONEST PRIOR: this is the program's WEAKEST topic (anchors:57,204); a real possible outcome is KILL-2 — that the continuous depth adds little over the already-released binary mask — which is itself a publishable, honest negative ('the binary mask is close to sufficient; here is the small residual where depth helps') and is reported as the headline, not hidden.

## Scope — out (what it does NOT claim)
- does NOT judge DANGER or planning consequence: an untrustworthy LABEL is not a dangerous SITUATION; that is dynfield (PLAN.md:124-137). Asserts label TRUST, never task risk.
- does NOT assert d(v) is CALIBRATED error probability: it RANKS untrust. Whether occupancy CONFIDENCE is honest in unobserved regions is vis-calibration (anchors:230-249) — different target, do not conflate.
- does NOT claim to RECOVER or RE-LABEL flagged voxels: it flags them; producing better GT is future work.
- does NOT claim cross-dataset transfer without re-measurement: SurroundOcc/OpenOccupancy use different reconstruction (Poisson vs TSDF) and masks; cross-dataset is a stated scope limit, direction-only at most (no cross-protocol number comparison; anchors:32-34).
- does NOT use the CAMERA visibility mask as evidence (circular); mask_camera appears only as a named negative control demonstrating the tautology.

## Threats to validity
- CIRCULAR ORACLE (the #1 threat, named at anchors:216-225): the score is a forward ray-cast; the camera mask is a forward ray-cast; agreement is tautological. NEUTRALIZED by dropping the camera-mask leg a priori, using mask_lidar as the only external reference (and only in smoke), and making H2's answer key the independent injected mask. A3 (sensor-origin ablation) is a built-in tripwire.
- INJECTION FAITHFULNESS (manufacture-the-oracle honesty, frontier rule 3): if synthetic injection does not resemble real reconstruction error (real error may be dominated by dynamic-object accumulation trails, not static occlusion), H2 proves detection-of-injected-errors, not detection-of-real-untrust. MITIGATED by the A4 injection-model ablation and by validating the injected-error spatial distribution against d-stratified dense-GT-vs-raw-LiDAR-sweep disagreement on held-out scenes; injection-only is stated as a scope limit if they diverge.
- MASK-TRAINED CHECKPOINT CONFOUND (H3): if the released checkpoint was TRAINED with the Occ3D visibility mask (loss masked to observed voxels), its errors already avoid unobserved voxels, so excluding d-flagged (mostly unobserved) voxels trivially does little — masking the result either way. SURFACED by reading each checkpoint's training protocol and reporting it; the A6 multi-checkpoint ablation exposes inconsistency.
- DISTANCE CONFOUND (correlation-as-causation): occlusion depth correlates with range; a naive d-result could be a range-result. NEUTRALIZED by B1 (distance baseline d must beat) and A5 (within-range-bin decoupling).
- RESOLUTION CEILING (structural, an upstream-data limit like occquery's 0.4 m bound, h3-findings:210-214): at 0.4 m voxels, thin occluders (a railing, a pole) may not register as occupied cells, so d(v) under-counts occlusion through thin structures. Stated as a structural boundary, not a softener; finer analysis needs raw-LiDAR re-voxelization (out of scope/v1).
- MINI HAS NO ERROR-RICH SCENES (external validity): the 10 local mini scenes are for instrument-hardening only (occquery's mini had 0 positives, h3-findings:107-108). Real numbers REQUIRE Occ3D val; a pre-declared val scene budget is part of the success criterion. No claim rests on mini.
- SYNTHETIC-vs-REAL (the house rule, PLAN.md:202-204): the injection benchmark is synthetic-by-construction; per the lab's own hardest-learned lesson (occquery synthetic F1=1.00 -> real precision 0/5, h3-findings:60-69), synthetic detection AP is NOT a real-world result. The synthetic arm proves the score has discriminative power on KNOWN errors; the external-validity bridge is H3 (real checkpoint) + the injection-faithfulness check. State this boundary explicitly in the conclusion.

## Argument chain
HYBRID inductive+deductive (frontier rule 6), with the camp declared. DEDUCTIVE SPINE (H1, H4): occlusion depth d(v) is a DERIVED geometric quantity — the count of occupied cells a ray from the ego pierces. By the construction of accumulate-then-reconstruct GT, a voxel at d(v)=0 is sensor-supported (a real return terminated there) while a voxel at d(v)>0 is filled by reconstruction THROUGH an occluder, so its label is a geometric extrapolation whose error must be non-decreasing in d (more occluders pierced = less direct evidence). This is a deduction from the labeling pipeline's geometry, not an empirical guess; it PREDICTS the AP-vs-d shape (rise then saturate, H4) BEFORE measurement — licensing the causal reading the way Why-Far-Looks-Up derives v(Z) prop 1/Z before testing. The non-identifiability witness (H1) is a quasi-PROOF: a binary mask assigns ONE value to a voxel, so two voxels equal in (mask,class,range) but different in d MUST be stamped identically by the mask while d distinguishes them — therefore d carries information the mask provably cannot, independent of any dataset. INDUCTIVE BRIDGE (H2, H3): the deductive spine says depth SHOULD predict error; the injected-error benchmark and the downstream exclusion study MEASURE whether it does, against controls engineered so a high score is NECESSARY but not SUFFICIENT — d must beat the d-SHUFFLED control (rules out marginal-distribution artifact), the DISTANCE baseline (rules out range-as-cause), and the BINARY MASK (rules out 'this is just observed-vs-unobserved'). FALLACY REFUSALS, by construction not disclaimer: affirming-the-consequent is blocked because a high AP alone is not claimed — only the gap over the shuffled/distance/mask controls (the remove-the-cause counterfactual); circular-oracle is blocked by dropping the camera mask and using an independent injected key; correlation-as-causation is blocked by B1+A5 (range decoupling) and the A3 origin tripwire; hasty-synthetic-to-real is blocked by labeling the injection benchmark synthetic and bridging to real via H3 + the injection-faithfulness check, per the lab's own occquery scar. CONCLUSION FORM (frontier rule 8, pick ONE lane): this is a DIAGNOSIS/INSTRUMENT paper in the DEFINE category — it ships (i) a measured GEOMETRIC fact (depth stratifies GT trust beyond the binary mask, proven by the size of the AP gap to controls) and (ii) a released audit TOOL, proven by a remove-it downstream gain (H3). It does NOT announce a benchmark and stop; it RESOLVES H1-H4 explicitly as confirmed/falsified/scoped, restates the RELATIVE d-vs-control gap (never an absolute AP) as load-bearing, and names the structural limits (0.4 m resolution, injection-faithfulness, mask-trained-checkpoint confound) as boundaries, not softeners. If the result is KILL-2 (binary mask nearly sufficient), the conclusion still resolves the hypothesis honestly — 'continuous depth adds a small, characterized residual over the released binary flag' — which is the kind of plain negative the lab's voice and the frontier corpus both prize.

Two independent blocker-grade fallacies sit at the root of the argument and were each reproduced on the actual code and Occ3D-nuScenes mini data, not merely argued from the prose: (1) the deductive spine's founding premise 'd(v)=0 = sensor-supported' is empirically false — P(observed|d=0)=0.24, P(d=0|observed)=0.47, agreement 0.62 — so occlusion_depth over the dense reconstructed field does not measure what the causal claim needs; and (2) the only 'load-bearing' oracle (H2 synthetic injection) is affirming-the-consequent — the answer key is a monotone function of d_true and the predictor equals d_true, yielding a guaranteed-large AP gap (reproduced 0.91 vs shuffled 0.60) that is informative about nothing in real GT. With H1+H2 void, the entire real-world burden falls on H3, which is gated on an unverified runnable checkpoint and confounded by mask-training that the plan only 'surfaces,' not controls — and the program is structured so every kill outcome is pre-declared publishable, making it operationally unfalsifiable. These are structural, root-level defects (the exact class of failure the global no-band-aid rule targets, and a re-run of the lab's own occquery synthetic-F1=1.00 -> real-0/5 scar), not fixable by tuning thresholds. A targeted revise is not enough: the predictor definition, the H2 oracle, and the causal framing must all be rebuilt before this is a sound paper. - **blocker**: FALSE DEDUCTIVE PREMISE — the spine is unsound. The entire 'DEDUCTIVE SPINE' (argument_chain) and H1/H4 rest on the axiom: 'a voxel at d(v)=0 is sensor-supported (a real return terminated there) while d(v)>0 is filled by reconstruction THROUGH an occluder ... licensing the causal reading.' This is empirically FALSE on the actual data. occlusion_depth (src/probe/raycast.py:91-102) counts OCCUPIED cells over the DENSE RECONSTRUCTED GT (map_occupancy with mask=None, occ3d.py:43-64), which has NOTHING to do with whether the sensor observed the voxel. Reproduced on data/gts (3 scenes, 7500-voxel sample, ego voxel (99,99,2)): P(observed=mask_lidar==1 | d==0) = 0.24, P(d==0 | observed) = 0.47, agreement(d==0 <-> observed) = 0.62. Most d==0 voxels are UNOBSERVED (no dense occupier on the ego->v ray, yet never sensed); a majority of observed voxels have d>0. So d(v) does NOT mean 'sensor-supported,' the deduction that 'error must be non-decreasing in d' loses its stated mechanism, and the claimed license for a causal reading (vs mere correlation) collapses. This is a deductive validity failure at the root, not a tunable parameter.
  - fix: Drop the 'd==0 = sensor-supported' premise; it is refuted by the data. Either (a) redefine the predictor as occlusion depth over the OBSERVED single-sweep field (occlusion through actually-sensed occupiers), making 'unobserved-because-occluded' a real geometric fact rather than a dense-field artifact, and re-derive the mechanism for THAT quantity; or (b) demote the entire paper from a DEDUCTIVE/causal 'occlusion predicts trust' claim to a purely correlational descriptive study with no causal language. As written the deductive spine must be deleted, which removes the paper's stated reason to license causation over correlation.
- **blocker**: AFFIRMING THE CONSEQUENT / CIRCULAR ORACLE in H2 — the single 'load-bearing' result is a tautology. The 'self-controlled injection oracle' the plan advertises as the cure for circularity IS the circularity. Injection key is defined as P(corrupt v) = monotone-increasing in d_true(v) (oracle section). The predictor d(v) = occlusion_depth over the (essentially clean) field == d_true(v) exactly on uncorrupted voxels (same function, same field). So the answer key is a monotone function of the predictor variable, and ranking by d(v) to recover the key is recovering f(d_true) by d_true. Reproduced numerically on scene-0061: AP(rank by d_true -> recover d_true-coupled injected key) = 0.908 vs AP(d-shuffled control) = 0.603 (= base rate). The 'load-bearing relative gap to the d-shuffled control' (success_criteria TIER-PUBLISHABLE-A, metrics PRIMARY) is GUARANTEED LARGE by construction and is informative about nothing in real GT. A high AP is logically necessary given the setup, so it cannot confirm the hypothesis — textbook affirming-the-consequent. The plan's claim 'H2's key is the INJECTED mask, independent of d(v) by construction' is false: the injection probability is a deterministic function of d_true = d(v).
  - fix: Break the key<->predictor dependence. The injection probability must NOT be a function of the predictor's own input. Options: (1) drive corruption from an INDEPENDENT physical proxy of reconstruction error (e.g. multi-sweep accumulation disagreement, dynamic-object trails, TSDF residual) measured WITHOUT occlusion_depth, then test whether d(v) detects THOSE errors; (2) if injection must be occlusion-coupled, the only admissible test is whether d(v) beats a DIFFERENT occlusion estimator computed from a held-out modality — never against a key that is a transform of d(v) itself. Until the key is constructed independently of d(v), H2 reports zero evidence and must be removed as the load-bearing result. The d-shuffled control does NOT rescue this: shuffling destroys the key's spatial binding too, so its low AP is also built-in.
- **major**: HASTY GENERALIZATION synthetic->real, reproducing the lab's own documented scar. The plan cites occquery (synthetic F1=1.00 -> real precision 0/5; docs/h3-real-data-findings.md:60-69 and the '0/5 precision' audit) as 'the precedent this plan refuses to repeat,' then reproduces its exact structure: a synthetic benchmark that succeeds by construction, with the ONLY non-tautological external evidence being H3. After defects 1-2, H1+H2 carry no real-world information, so the entire empirical weight falls on H3 — which the plan itself rates dependent on an unconfirmed CPU-runnable released checkpoint (dependencies: 'PROVISIONAL in the ledger; verify existence', benchmark-anchors flags OccNL/ReliOcc/GS-Occ3D code as stub-or-unverified) and exposed to the mask-trained confound (next defect). 'Synthetic detection AP proves the score has discriminative power on KNOWN errors' is precisely the inference occquery's scar shows to be void.
  - fix: State plainly that no real-world claim survives without H3, gate the whole paper on H3 succeeding on a VERIFIED, runnable checkpoint (name it, confirm it runs CPU-only or secure GPU before pre-registration), and forbid any framing in which a synthetic AP is presented as evidence the score works. If H3 cannot be run, the paper is not viable and should not proceed past smoke.
- **major**: H3 ORACLE SHARES THE PREDICTOR'S FAILURE MODE — the central confound is 'surfaced,' not controlled. threats_to_validity 'MASK-TRAINED CHECKPOINT CONFOUND' concedes: if the checkpoint was trained with the Occ3D visibility mask (loss masked to observed voxels), its errors already avoid unobserved voxels. Since d(v)>0 voxels are disproportionately unobserved (and the dense-field d>0 set skews unobserved per the reproduced contingency), excluding top-d(v) voxels moves mIoU/RayIoU for a reason ENTIRELY unrelated to 'label trust' — it removes voxels the model was trained to ignore. The plan's only mitigation is 'read each checkpoint's training protocol and report it' (A6). Reporting a confound is not removing it. The matched control (B3) matches on BINARY visibility, but within each visibility stratum d(v) still correlates with unobservedness/range/clutter, so the match cannot decouple the very factor that drives the downstream delta. This makes H3 a correlation-as-causation result: 'flagged labels are where the model fails' is confounded by 'flagged labels are where the model was trained not to look.'
  - fix: Require at least one released checkpoint trained WITHOUT mask-masking (dense supervision) as the primary H3 arm; if none exists, H3 cannot support the trust claim and must be dropped. Add a control that holds observed/unobserved EXACTLY fixed (e.g. test d(v) ranking strictly WITHIN the observed stratum, where mask-training gives no free pass) — if the downstream delta vanishes there, the H3 signal was the training confound, not trust.
- **major**: UNFALSIFIABLE IN PRACTICE — every outcome is pre-declared publishable, so no result can refute the program. KILL-1 (chance) -> 'reported as the headline, honest negative'; KILL-2 (depth redundant with binary mask) -> 'PIVOT to a calibrated descriptive study / fold into vis-calibration'; KILL-3 (downstream null) -> 'keep H1+H2 as a GT-geometry result only.' expected_results even pre-commits 'a real possible outcome is KILL-2 ... which is itself a publishable, honest negative.' A hypothesis structure in which confirmation, partial-confirmation, and every flavor of disconfirmation are all declared a publishable result is operationally unfalsifiable: there is no observation the authors would accept as 'this paper should not exist.' That is the inverse failure to a normal unfalsifiable claim, but it is the same logical defect — the conclusion is immune to the evidence.
  - fix: Pre-register ONE outcome that ends the paper with no publication: e.g. 'if d(v) does not beat the binary mask as a ranker (KILL-2), the project stops — it is not reframed as descriptive, because the binary mask already exists and a residual-characterization is not a contribution at a top venue.' A real kill must mean abandonment, not relabeling. Tie the publish decision to a single load-bearing comparison fixed in advance.
- **minor**: H1 'non-identifiability witness' proves non-constancy, not information-about-trust — overstated as a 'quasi-PROOF.' The witness ('two voxels equal in (mask,class,range) but different d(v): the binary mask MUST stamp them identically, d distinguishes them ... therefore d carries information the mask provably cannot') is logically valid only for the claim 'd is not a function of (mask,class,range).' That is true of ANY voxel-indexed real quantity not measurable w.r.t. those covariates — including a per-voxel random number, which also 'distinguishes' the two voxels. Distinguishing voxels is not the same as carrying information about LABEL TRUST. The plan elevates this to a near-proof of the contribution (argument_chain: 'a quasi-PROOF ... independent of any dataset'), which is a non-sequitur.
  - fix: Demote H1 from 'proof that d carries trust information' to 'd is not redundant with the binary-mask covariates' (a necessary, far-from-sufficient condition). The trust content must come from an independent error oracle (see defect 2 fix); state explicitly that non-identifiability alone is consistent with d being pure noise.
- **minor**: RESIDUAL CORRELATION-AS-CAUSATION: B1+A5 decouple range but not ray clutter. d(v) co-varies with the total dense-GT occupied mass along the ego->v segment (scene clutter / proximity to large structures), which is an uncontrolled common cause of BOTH d(v) and reconstruction error (cluttered regions are both more occluded AND harder to reconstruct). Controlling for Euclidean range (B1) and within-range bins (A5) does not control for along-ray occupied mass. So even a clean H2/H3 gap over those controls could reflect 'this ray traverses a dense region,' not 'occlusion structure causes untrust.'
  - fix: Add a control that holds along-ray occupied mass (or local occupied density at v) fixed and asks whether d(v) still stratifies error; without it, any causal phrasing ('occlusion structure, not mere farness, predicts untrust') is unsupported and must be softened to correlation.
