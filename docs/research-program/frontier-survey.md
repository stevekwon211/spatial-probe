# Frontier survey — papers + shared patterns

Per-domain frontier papers from the 2026-06-21 survey, with the methodology each shares. Verified against actual papers (titles/venues/arXiv IDs), not invented.

## Autonomous-driving 3D semantic occupancy prediction and occupancy flow (camera/LiDAR/multi

**Papers:**
- Occ3D: A Large-Scale 3D Occupancy Prediction Benchmark for Autonomous Driving (NeurIPS 2023 Datasets & Benchmarks (arXiv:2304.14365))
- SurroundOcc: Multi-Camera 3D Occupancy Prediction for Autonomous Driving (ICCV 2023 (arXiv:2303.09551))
- OpenOccupancy: A Large Scale Benchmark for Surrounding Semantic Occupancy Perception (ICCV 2023 (arXiv:2303.03991))
- FB-OCC: 3D Occupancy Prediction based on Forward-Backward View Transformation (CVPR 2023 Workshop technical report (challenge-winning solution) (arXiv:2307.01492))
- Fully Sparse 3D Occupancy Prediction (SparseOcc) + RayIoU (ECCV 2024 (MCG-NJU) (arXiv:2312.17118))
- OccWorld: Learning a 3D Occupancy World Model for Autonomous Driving (ECCV 2024 (arXiv:2311.16038))
- Cam4DOcc: Benchmark for Camera-Only 4D Occupancy Forecasting in Autonomous Driving Applications (CVPR 2024 (arXiv:2311.17663))
- Occupancy Flow Fields for Motion Forecasting in Autonomous Driving (IEEE RA-L 2022 / Waymo (seeds the Waymo Open Dataset Occupancy-and-Flow Challenge, CVPR 2022) (arXiv:2203.03875))

**Shared patterns:**
- Oracle = accumulate-then-reconstruct, never hand-drawn voxels. Every occupancy paper builds dense GT by aggregating multi-frame LiDAR (dynamic objects in object/box frame, static scene in global frame) and filling holes via surface reconstruction (Occ3D=VDBFusion/TSDF, SurroundOcc=Poisson, OpenOccupancy=pseudo-label augment + ~4000h human purify). The 'ground truth' is itself a model output, so it inherits LiDAR sparsity, pose error, and reconstruction bias — a structural circularity the field rarely flags.
- Visibility/observed-vs-occluded masking is load-bearing and protocol-defining. Occ3D scores only camera-visible voxels; Waymo Occupancy-Flow separates observed vs occluded occupancy; SparseOcc shows the visible-mask voxel-mIoU is gameable (rewards thick surfaces, conflates occupancy with depth). What counts as 'unobserved' silently sets the difficulty and the metric, and is the single biggest source of cross-paper incomparability.
- Success is almost never pre-registered as a numeric threshold internally — it is defined either post-hoc (beat the prior SOTA mIoU) or externally by a challenge leaderboard. The cleanest pre-registration comes from competitions: FB-OCC (CVPR'23 Occupancy Challenge, mIoU fixed) and the Waymo Occupancy-and-Flow Challenge. Benchmark papers (Occ3D, OpenOccupancy, Cam4DOcc) pre-declare the *task/metric/split* but not a passing bar.
- Metric framing is dominantly RELATIVE-on-a-shared-oracle: report absolute mIoU/IoU but argue via deltas against last year's method on the same fixed dataset. This makes results internally rankable but means absolute numbers (e.g., OpenOccupancy ~10-20 mIoU vs Occ3D ~28) are NOT comparable across papers because the GT, grid resolution, class set, and visibility mask differ.
- Ablations are cumulative 'roadmap ladders' (start from a baseline, add one component per row, report monotonic metric gains) rather than factorial/leave-one-out designs. FB-OCC (23.12->54.19 mIoU step-by-step) and Cam4DOcc (four baseline families from static-world up to end-to-end) are archetypal. This proves each addition helps *given the others* but rarely isolates interactions or shows where a component hurts.
- Argument type is uniformly inductive/empirical-by-leaderboard; deduction appears only at the metric-design layer. The strongest scientific moves are not new accuracy numbers but new *measurement* constructs: SparseOcc's RayIoU (ray-cast, first-hit class+depth, kills thick-surface inflation) and Waymo's flow-traced occupancy (occupancy must be consistent with flow by construction). Reforming the metric is how this field actually advances its epistemics.
- Limitations are stated weakly and kill conditions are nearly absent. Most papers list at most an implicit failure (distance degradation: FB-OCC, OccWorld decay curves, Cam4DOcc horizon table) and no condition under which the central claim would be falsified. The two honest exceptions are SparseOcc (cascading error: discarded empty voxels are unrecoverable, confining the mask transformer to bad geometry) and OccWorld (cannot forecast newly-appearing agents; 'beats GT on drivable area' as a tacit oracle-quality admission).
- Distance/horizon decay is the universal unstated adversary. Camera occupancy degrades with range (FB-OCC explicitly), and forecasting degrades with time-to-future (OccWorld 1/2/3s, Cam4DOcc 0.5->2.0s, Waymo up to 8s). Several papers average over horizons in the headline number, which hides the decay; the more rigorous ones (OccWorld, Cam4DOcc, Waymo) report per-horizon curves that function as a soft falsification surface.
- Conclusion form is contribution-announcement + community-aspiration, not hypothesis-resolution. Papers conclude with 'first benchmark/network', a SOTA number, a code-release promise, and 'we hope this boosts the field' (OpenOccupancy, SparseOcc, Occ3D). Forecasting/world-model papers (OccWorld, Cam4DOcc, Waymo Occupancy-Flow) add a prescriptive note (recommended supervision: 3D flow, inflated GMO, flow-trace consistency).
- Joint representation as the recurring thesis: the frontier keeps arguing that a *richer joint field* beats decoupled outputs — occupancy+semantics > boxes (Occ3D/SurroundOcc), occupancy+flow > trajectories-or-occupancy-alone (Waymo, Cam4DOcc), and generative occupancy world models > perception-only (OccWorld). The shared methodological bet is that fine-grained, geometry-complete, motion-aware voxel fields are the right substrate, paid for by expensive reconstructed oracles and metrics that must police consistency.

## Scene/scenario retrieval and spatial query languages for autonomous vehicles (AV): languag

**Papers:**
- RefAV: Towards Planning-Centric Scenario Mining (NeurIPS 2025 (Spotlight); arXiv:2505.20981, May 2025. Davidson, Ramanan, Peri (CMU).)
- Language Prompt for Autonomous Driving (NuPrompt benchmark + PromptTrack baseline) (AAAI 2024; arXiv:2309.04379, Sep 2023. Wu et al.)
- NuScenes-QA: A Multi-modal Visual Question Answering Benchmark for Autonomous Driving Scenario (AAAI 2024; arXiv:2305.14836, May 2023 (v2 Feb 2024). Qian et al.)
- NuScenes-SpatialQA: A Spatial Understanding and Reasoning Benchmark for Vision-Language Models in Autonomous Driving (arXiv:2504.03164, Apr 2025 (v1/v2). Tian et al. (TACO group). No confirmed conference acceptance found.)
- Talk2Car: Taking Control of Your Self-Driving Car (EMNLP 2019; arXiv:1909.10838, Sep 2019. Deruyttere et al. (KU Leuven).)
- Technical Report for Argoverse2 Scenario Mining Challenges on Iterative Error Correction and Spatially-Aware Prompting (CVPR 2025 Workshop on Autonomous Driving (AV2 Scenario Mining Challenge); arXiv:2506.11124, Jun 2025.)

**Shared patterns:**
- Compositional 'spatial query language' as the expressivity primitive: the strongest systems (RefAV/RefProg, the CVPR'25 report) define expressivity as a CLOSED set of typed atomic predicates — object state (turning, accelerating, stationary), pairwise scene-graph relations (being_crossed_by, facing_toward, near_objects), and map context (at_pedestrian_crossing, on_intersection) — closed under boolean operators (scenario_and/or/not) and relation reversal. NL is mapped to this language by LLM PROGRAM SYNTHESIS rather than end-to-end grounding. Expressivity-vs-baseline is argued as composition-of-predicates > monolithic VLM/CLIP embeddings.
- Oracle is template/scene-graph-derived from existing 3D human annotations, not freshly hand-labeled per query. Every benchmark (RefAV, nuScenes-QA, NuScenes-SpatialQA, NuPrompt, Talk2Car) bootstraps ground truth from pre-existing 3D detection/track annotations + HD maps, then layers language via templates (nuScenes-QA: 66 templates; NuScenes-SpatialQA: auto scene graph) or human-in-the-loop element-then-GPT-rewrite (NuPrompt) or crowd commands with 2-round verification (Talk2Car). Consequence: the hypothesis space and the oracle often share a vocabulary (RefAV uses the same 28-function API to label and to infer).
- None are formally pre-registered; success is operationalized as 'beat baselines / beat a trivial floor / approach an upper bound,' and increasingly EXTERNALIZED via a public challenge. RefAV and the CVPR'25 report convert success criteria into a leaderboard (official AV2 test set, fixed HOTA-Temporal protocol), which substitutes for pre-registration as the commitment device. Talk2Car and nuScenes-QA instead anchor to explicit floors (random/biggest-box) and ceilings (GT-feature upper bound 84.3%).
- Absolute primary number, relative deltas underneath, with deliberate separation of perception error from reasoning/grounding error. Reported headline is absolute (HOTA-Temporal, Top-1 accuracy, IoU>0.5, AMOTA), but the persuasive work is done by reporting results under MULTIPLE track/box sources (RefAV: GT vs LE3DE2E vs LT3D; nuScenes-QA: GT boxes 84.3% vs detected −0.6%) to attribute error to the right stage. Task-specific metric variants are invented to localize claims in time (HOTA-Temporal vs HOTA-Track) or magnitude (Tolerance-based Accuracy for metric distances).
- LLM/model-swap is now a standard ablation axis, reported with a failure/robustness rate, not just accuracy. RefAV and the CVPR'25 report both ablate the program-synthesizing LLM (Claude vs Gemini vs Qwen) and report a program-FAILURE / fault rate alongside HOTA-Temporal; NuScenes-SpatialQA ablates generic-vs-spatial-enhanced VLMs across question categories. The other canonical ablation is the representation/track-source axis (GT vs detected boxes; fusion method; reasoning depth).
- Evidence is inductive/empirical (leaderboards + ablation grids) wrapped in a deductive design scaffold ('AV scenarios are relational+temporal ⇒ a typed compositional API is the right hypothesis space' / 'data scarcity is the bottleneck ⇒ scale the data'). Pure-method reports (CVPR'25 2506.11124) are deductive in mechanism (rule-based error-feedback + prompt engineering) but validated inductively by deltas.
- Coverage ceiling stated as the honest limitation / partial kill condition: the closed query vocabulary cannot express some prompts (RefAV: weather/lighting/appearance have no atomic function), template generation limits language diversity (nuScenes-QA), compositional 'unified' prompts degrade accuracy (NuPrompt 0.242 vs 0.271), and automatic pipelines need manual code edits beyond K=5 iterations (CVPR'25). Each paper names the structural failure mode rather than claiming the task solved.
- Conclusion form is uniformly 'constructive frontier-marker,' never closure. All six ship a dataset/method, report a best number that leaves large explicit headroom (RefAV 50.1/100; Talk2Car best <51% IoU; nuScenes-QA 60.4% vs 84.3% ceiling), attribute the gap to a named capability (counting, metric estimation, multi-agent relations, compositional language), and hand the open problem forward — often literally via a workshop challenge.

## Robotics geometric mapping and motion-planning: free-space / configuration-space represent

**Papers:**
- Voxblox: Incremental 3D Euclidean Signed Distance Fields for On-Board MAV Planning (IROS 2017 (arXiv:1611.03631), Oleynikova, Taylor, Fehr, Siegwart, Nieto (ETH ASL))
- FIESTA: Fast Incremental Euclidean Distance Fields for Online Motion Planning of Aerial Robots (IROS 2019 (arXiv:1903.02144), Han, Gao, Zhou, Shen (HKUST Aerial Robotics))
- nvblox: GPU-Accelerated Incremental Signed Distance Field Mapping (RA-L / ICRA 2024 (arXiv:2311.00626), Millane, Oleynikova, Wirbel, Steiner, Ramasamy, Tingdahl, et al. (NVIDIA))
- Voxfield: Non-Projective Signed Distance Fields for Online Planning and 3D Reconstruction (IROS 2022, Pan, Kompis, Bartolomei, Mascaro, Stachniss, Chli (ETH VIS4ROB / Uni Bonn))
- Computing Large Convex Regions of Obstacle-Free Space Through Semidefinite Programming (IRIS) (WAFR 2014 (Springer STAR 2015), Deits, Tedrake (MIT CSAIL))
- Certified Polyhedral Decompositions of Collision-Free Configuration Space (C-IRIS) (IJRR 2024 (arXiv:2302.12219), Dai, Amice, Werner, Zhang, Tedrake (TRI / MIT CSAIL))
- Hamilton-Jacobi Reachability: A Brief Overview and Recent Advances (IEEE CDC 2017 (arXiv:1709.07523), Bansal, Chen, Herbert, Tomlin (UC Berkeley / Stanford))

**Shared patterns:**
- TWO METHODOLOGICAL CAMPS SHARING ONE OBJECT. All seven describe the geometry of collision-free configuration/work space, but split on what 'validated' means. Inductive camp (Voxblox, FIESTA, nvblox, Voxfield): correctness = MEASURED distance-field error vs an externally constructed ground-truth field on benchmark datasets. Deductive camp (IRIS, C-IRIS, HJ reachability): correctness = a PROVEN property (separating-hyperplane construction, SOS nonnegativity certificate, or HJI PDE value function), with no dataset and no error metric. The phrase 'ground truth' only exists in the inductive camp; in the deductive camp the construction/proof IS the truth.
- ORACLE = EXACT GEOMETRIC RECOMPUTATION, NOT HUMAN LABELS. Every inductive mapping paper builds its own ground-truth distance field by an exact brute-force computation over known geometry: Voxblox rasterizes a mesh and computes the exact Euclidean distance transform; FIESTA builds a K-D tree over final occupancy and queries exact nearest-obstacle distance; nvblox computes voxel-center-to-dataset-surface distance; Voxfield evaluates distance RMSE on a regular 3D grid against known scene geometry. The oracle is deterministic and re-derivable, which is the field's reproducibility backbone — there is no annotation ambiguity, the ground truth is the math.
- HEADLINE CLAIMS ARE RELATIVE; THE UNDERLYING QUANTITY IS ABSOLUTE. The inductive papers all headline a comparison against the immediately prior named method (FIESTA: 'order of magnitude over Voxblox'; nvblox: '177x / 31x'; Voxfield: '-32% error, -42% ESDF time'; Voxblox: 'faster than Octomap, more accurate than occupancy'), yet each underlying metric (RMSE in meters or voxels, ms, coverage %) is an absolute geometric/physical quantity. The relative framing is rhetorical (beat the predecessor); the absolute oracle comparison is what makes the result checkable. The deductive papers are the inverse: absolute/binary on the guarantee (free-or-not, safe-or-not, certain), relative only on secondary size/runtime.
- NO PRE-REGISTRATION ANYWHERE; SUCCESS BARS ARE EMERGENT. None of the seven pre-registers a hypothesis or commits to a numeric pass/fail threshold before experiments. In the inductive camp the bar is implicitly 'strictly dominate the prior representation on accuracy and speed, and close the loop on real/simulated hardware' (Voxblox/FIESTA/nvblox/Voxfield all add an on-board or simulated planning demo as the systems-level success signal). In the deductive camp the 'success criterion' is fixed by the mathematics (a sound certificate / exact set) before any experiment runs — so pre-registration is moot for them, but absent for the empirical ones.
- SHARED CANONICAL BENCHMARKS + OPEN SOURCE = THE REPRODUCIBILITY NORM. The same few datasets recur as a de-facto standard (Cow-and-Lady RGB-D with structured-light reference appears in Voxblox, FIESTA, Voxfield; MaiCity/KITTI LiDAR and EuRoC stereo recur; Replica/Redwood in nvblox), and every system ships open-source code (voxblox, FIESTA, nvblox, voxfield, IRIS/C-IRIS in Drake). Validation = run the public code on the shared dataset against the recomputed exact field. Reproducibility is achieved by released-code + common-dataset + deterministic-oracle, not by statistical replication or significance testing (no confidence intervals or p-values appear; results are point estimates and dataset averages).
- ABLATION ISOLATES THE GEOMETRIC MECHANISM, NOT HYPERPARAMETERS-AT-LARGE. The strongest ablations target the one geometric idea: Voxblox quasi-Euclidean vs full-Euclidean propagation; FIESTA neighbor-connectivity {6,18,26,24,32} and indexing/block-size; Voxfield projective vs non-projective TSDF integration; nvblox ablates by hardware platform (desktop/laptop/Jetson) to prove portability rather than by kernel. Deductive papers 'ablate' by capability/scope (C-IRIS vs IRIS = removing the convex-C-space-obstacle restriction; IRIS vs seed placement; HJ full-grid vs decomposition). Across both camps the ablation answers 'is the headlined mechanism actually responsible,' not generic tuning.
- LIMITATIONS ENCODE A FUNDAMENTAL EXACTNESS-VS-TRACTABILITY TRADE, AND THE FRONTIER IS DRIVEN BY IT. The two camps are mirror images. Inductive methods buy speed/online-operation by accepting bounded INEXACTNESS that they then measure (FIESTA states outright that 'all BFS-based ESDF updates cannot be accurate'; Voxblox's quasi-Euclidean and truncation limits; degradation in unobserved space). Deductive methods buy EXACTNESS/soundness by accepting tractability and scope limits (HJ's exponential curse of dimensionality; IRIS's local-optimality + no-completeness + convex-obstacle assumption; C-IRIS's bounded-rotation joints + convex semi-algebraic geometry + DOF scaling). Notably NONE has a true falsification 'kill condition': empirical papers report error rather than a threshold that would condemn the method, and proof-carrying papers cannot be empirically killed because the property is theorem-backed (a wrong dynamics/geometry MODEL degrades the guarantee, but the algorithm does not 'fail a test'). The honest limitation statements (FIESTA's exactness admission, IRIS's no-completeness, HJ's dimensionality) substitute for an explicit kill criterion.
- ARGUMENT TYPE CORRELATES WITH SUBFIELD AND WITH WHO BUILT IT. Distance-field mapping (perception-adjacent, ETH/HKUST/NVIDIA robotics-systems groups) argues INDUCTIVELY: measure error+timing, generalize to 'better representation,' ship code. Free-space/safety geometry (planning-and-control-theory, MIT/TRI/Berkeley/Stanford) argues DEDUCTIVELY: prove the property (separating hyperplane / SOS / HJI PDE), then demonstrate scalability. The systems camp's contribution is an artifact-plus-benchmark; the theory camp's contribution is a guarantee-plus-construction. A research program that wants BOTH (e.g. a planning-grade ESDF with certified distance bounds) sits in the gap none of these fully closes — the empirical fields are fast but uncertified, and the certified geometries are sound but either offline, low-DOF, or scope-restricted.
- CONCLUSION FORM MATCHES ARGUMENT TYPE: 'we built X that beats Y' vs 'we proved property P is achievable.' Inductive papers conclude with a feasibility/dominance statement anchored on the open-source system and the relative metric (Voxblox: real-time on-board ESDF is feasible; FIESTA: dominates Voxblox; nvblox: faster on GPU incl. embedded; Voxfield: strictly improves the Voxblox backbone). Deductive papers conclude with an existence-of-guarantee statement (IRIS: large provably-free convex regions, efficiently; C-IRIS: first rigorous certified C-free decomposition; HJ: exact formal safety, gated by dimensionality). The empirical conclusions are revisable by the next faster system; the formal conclusions are permanent given their assumptions.

## 3D-perception benchmark & evaluation methodology: oracle construction, robustness/sensitiv

**Papers:**
- Benchmark Designers Should "Train on the Test Set" to Expose Exploitable Non-Visual Shortcuts (arXiv 2025 (cs.CV), arXiv:2511.04655 — Brown, J. Yang, S. Yang, Fergus, Xie (NYU VisionX))
- Why Far Looks Up: Probing Spatial Representation in Vision-Language Models (arXiv 2026, arXiv:2605.30161 — Min, Jung, D. Lee, Jeon, Y. Su, Tremblay, C.H. Song, Park (SNU / NVIDIA / OSU))
- On Offline Evaluation of 3D Object Detection for Autonomous Driving (ICCV 2023 Workshops (BRAVO), arXiv:2308.12779 — Schreier, Renz, Geiger, Chitta (Univ. Tübingen, AVG))
- Learning to Evaluate Perception Models Using Planner-Centric Metrics (CVPR 2020, arXiv:2004.08745 — Philion, Kar, Fidler (NVIDIA / Univ. Toronto / Vector Institute))
- Robo3D: Towards Robust and Reliable 3D Perception against Corruptions (ICCV 2023, arXiv:2303.17597 — Kong, Y. Liu, X. Li, R. Chen, W. Zhang, Ren, Pan, K. Chen, Z. Liu (NTU S-Lab / Shanghai AI Lab))
- Benchmarking Robustness of 3D Object Detection to Common Corruptions in Autonomous Driving (CVPR 2023, arXiv:2303.11040 — Dong, Kang, J. Zhang, Z. Zhu, Y. Wang, X. Yang, H. Su, X. Wei, J. Zhu (Tsinghua TSAIL / RealAI))
- Thinking in Space: How Multimodal Large Language Models See, Remember, and Recall Spaces (VSI-Bench) (CVPR 2025, arXiv:2412.14171 — J. Yang, S. Yang, Gupta, Han, Fei-Fei Li, Xie (NYU / Yale / Stanford))
- Investigating Domain Gaps for Indoor 3D Object Detection (ACM MM 2025, arXiv:2508.17439 — Z. Zhao, Z. Xu, Q. Chen, Y. Peng, Y. Liu (Peking University))

**Shared patterns:**
- Distrust the absolute headline number; report it RELATIVE to a controlled reference. Every paper converts raw accuracy into a comparison: normalized degradation (Robo3D mCE/mRR, Dong mCE), a gap to a vision-blind solver (2511.04655) or to a human oracle (VSI-Bench 33.8 pt), a counterfactual accuracy gap Δ (Why Far Looks Up), a correlation-to-driving (Schreier), or a directional A→B matrix vs same-domain oracle (indoor domain-gap). Clean/absolute accuracy is explicitly framed as necessary-but-insufficient and prone to over-claiming.
- Build a CONTROLLED oracle where exactly one factor varies, instead of using found data. Recurs as: physically-principled corruption simulation at graded severities with preserved GT (Robo3D, Dong); synthetic scenes that decorrelate a confound (SpatialTunnel decouples vertical-position from depth; ProcFront decouples layout from instance); QA auto-generated from reconstruction ground truth so the label is exact (VSI-Bench); a fixed downstream stack so only the detector varies (Schreier); a planner conditioned on GT trajectories (PKL). The controlled stressor IS the methodological contribution.
- Name and neutralize the dataset-bias / shortcut confound up front, often adversarially. Train a text-only model on the test set to expose non-visual solvability (2511.04655); hold taxonomy fixed and exclude rare/structural objects to isolate visual shift (indoor domain-gap, VSI-Bench QC); remove the natural height↔distance correlation (Why Far Looks Up); report frequency baselines so 'most-frequent-answer' performance is visible (VSI-Bench). The shortcut is treated as the default hypothesis to be ruled out, not an afterthought.
- Report NEGATIVE and cautionary results as first-class findings — the main anti-over-claiming move. CoT/self-consistency/ToT make spatial accuracy WORSE (VSI-Bench); planner-centric metrics should not be trusted exclusively (Schreier, cautioning the very PKL line of work); camera-only detection is brittle (Dong); few-shot adaptation stays far below oracle (indoor domain-gap). None of these are buried; they are the headline.
- Make a SPECIFIC, falsifiable prediction tied to a measurable threshold or sign, rather than a vague 'X is better.' Counter-perspective accuracy must drop and Δ>0 / VD-EI>0 (Why Far Looks Up, even derived from perspective geometry); the vision-blind gap must WIDEN after debiasing (2511.04655); mCE must differ across representations or the robustness claim fails (Robo3D); NDS must out-correlate mAP with driving (Schreier). The crispest papers pre-commit to what a falsifying result would look like.
- Establish validity by a SWEEP/population, then aggregate — almost all arguments are inductive over many models/conditions, not single-system demos. 16 detectors (Schreier), 24+ detectors x 27 corruptions (Dong), corruption suites across 4 datasets (Robo3D), 4 benchmarks (2511.04655), model families x data scales (Why Far Looks Up), six controlled cross-domain pairs (indoor). Generalization is earned from the population; one or two papers add a deductive geometric/metric core (PKL's planning objective, Why-Far's projection law) to license the prediction before testing it.
- Pre-registration is essentially ABSENT across the frontier (none of the 8 pre-register), but its function is approximated by other means: pre-committed metric definitions (MRA's threshold-averaging; mCE/mRR normalization), explicitly enumerated kill conditions (Why Far Looks Up lists three; VSI-Bench's CoT result; indoor's 'if adaptation can't close the gap'), and adversarial self-checks (train-on-test). The gap between this field and the NeurIPS pre-registration / psychology-reproducibility agenda is the single clearest opportunity for a lab that wants to lead on rigor.
- Frame the contribution as INFRASTRUCTURE/DIAGNOSIS with honest scope, not as 'we solved it.' Robo3D, Dong, VSI-Bench, and the indoor domain-gap paper all ship a benchmark/dataset + baselines and explicitly call for future work; 2511.04655 ships a debiasing PROCEDURE; Schreier ships a metric-validity finding; Why Far Looks Up ships a probing method. The conclusion form is consistently 'here is a better way to measure / a flaw to avoid,' which structurally limits over-claiming.

## Spatial AI / embodied + vision-language spatial reasoning (CVPR, CoRL, RSS, NeurIPS, ICRA)

**Papers:**
- SpatialVLM: Endowing Vision-Language Models with Spatial Reasoning Capabilities (CVPR 2024 (arXiv:2401.12168, Jan 2024) — Google DeepMind)
- RT-2: Vision-Language-Action Models Transfer Web Knowledge to Robotic Control (CoRL 2023 (PMLR v229) / arXiv:2307.15818 — Google DeepMind)
- Diffusion Policy: Visuomotor Policy Learning via Action Diffusion (RSS 2023 (also IJRR 2024) / arXiv:2303.04137 — Columbia, TRI, MIT)
- SpatialRGPT: Grounded Spatial Reasoning in Vision-Language Models (NeurIPS 2024 / arXiv:2406.01584 — UC San Diego, NVIDIA)
- OpenVLA: An Open-Source Vision-Language-Action Model (CoRL 2024 / arXiv:2406.09246 — Stanford, UC Berkeley, TRI, Google DeepMind)
- ConceptGraphs: Open-Vocabulary 3D Scene Graphs for Perception and Planning (ICRA 2024 / arXiv:2309.16650 — U. Toronto, U. Montréal, MIT, UMass, JHU)

**Shared patterns:**
- HYPOTHESIS FORM is almost always a single deficiency-attribution or mechanism claim, not a vague aim. The strongest version names the missing ingredient or the property prior work lacks, then intervenes and measures: SpatialVLM ('limited spatial reasoning is due to lack of 3D data in training'), Diffusion Policy ('data needs multimodality+high-dim+stable training; diffusion has all three'), ConceptGraphs ('dense per-point maps fail scalability+structure; object-centric graph fixes both'). The claim is engineered to be falsifiable by a single comparison.
- PRE-REGISTRATION IS ABSENT EVERYWHERE. None of the 6 pre-registers hypotheses or criteria (typical for ML/robotics venues). The substitute for pre-registration is (a) a FIXED evaluation budget declared up front (RT-2 ~6k trials; OpenVLA 170+60 rollouts; Diffusion Policy 12 tasks x 4 benchmarks), and (b) an EXPLICIT, pre-stated success threshold for noisy continuous targets (SpatialVLM half-to-twice / 0.5x–2x; SpatialRGPT ±25%). Choosing the tolerance band before reporting is the field's informal pre-registration analogue.
- TWO-TIER ORACLE is the dominant pattern for spatial/embodied work: a cheap, scalable, NOISY oracle for TRAINING (auto-generated from detection+metric-depth+geometry — SpatialVLM, SpatialRGPT) and a smaller, trusted, HUMAN-or-3D-GEOMETRY oracle for EVALUATION (held-out human-annotated VQA; Omni3D cuboids; AMT caption labels; physical-robot success). Papers explicitly acknowledge the training oracle is noisy and design an experiment (e.g. SpatialVLM's Gaussian-noise ablation) to prove the noise is tolerable.
- METRIC FRAMING is deliberately chosen to match noise level. Continuous spatial quantities are converted to PASS/FAIL via a tolerance band (within-2x, within-25%) rather than reported as raw error, because absolute metric error on noisy labels is unreliable. Headline numbers are then often RELATIVE multipliers (RT-2 '~2x/~6x', Diffusion Policy 'average 46.9% improvement', OpenVLA '7x fewer params') because relative gains survive task-to-task scale differences — but the relative summary is paired with absolute per-task tables for auditability.
- ABLATIONS ARE MECE AND CLAIM-MAPPED: each ablation isolates ONE factor and maps back to exactly one sub-claim or design decision. The recurring axes are (1) capacity/scale (model size), (2) training recipe (scratch vs fine-tune vs co-fine-tune; frozen vs unfrozen encoder), (3) representation/architecture (CNN vs Transformer; RGB vs RGB+depth; AABB vs OBB; fused vs single encoder), and (4) data quality (injected noise; metric-scale supervision). A NULL or NEGATIVE ablation is reported when it justifies a cheaper choice (OpenVLA 224 vs 384 'no difference'; ConceptGraphs LLM-vs-greedy association 'minuscule improvement') — used as evidence FOR a design decision, not buried.
- ARGUMENT TYPE is predominantly INDUCTIVE/empirical (scale up, measure, beat baselines), but the best papers add a near-DEDUCTIVE 'why it should work' scaffold derived from first principles or from named failure modes of alternatives (Diffusion Policy deriving 3 desiderata and citing EBM instability; SpatialRGPT/SpatialVLM grounding label correctness in projective geometry; ConceptGraphs deriving required representation properties). DEDUCTIVE reasoning shows up inside the method as composed chain-of-thought (RT-2, SpatialVLM) rather than as the paper's overall proof structure. Emergence claims (RT-2) are framed as EXISTENCE PROOFS plus an ablation that removes the cause.
- LIMITATIONS ARE A DEDICATED, PLAIN-SPOKEN SECTION (Sec 5 RT-2, Sec VIII Diffusion Policy, Sec 6 OpenVLA, Sec H ConceptGraphs) and they FENCE the headline claim to its real scope. The most rigorous limitation names a structural boundary the method CANNOT cross (RT-2: 'does not acquire any ability to perform new motions ... limited to skills seen in robot data') or an upstream oracle/label ceiling that caps achievable accuracy (SpatialRGPT AABB labels; ConceptGraphs LVLM-caption errors propagating into planning). FALSIFIABLE KILL CONDITIONS are rarely formalized as such; instead a deliberately adversarial ablation (noise injection, velocity control, long horizon, AABB-vs-OBB, hard-unseen splits) plays the role of the stress test that the result had to survive — failing it would have killed the claim.
- CONCLUSION FORM is consistently STRONG-CLAIM-THEN-FENCE: restate the hypothesis as confirmed, summarize what was ablated to earn it, then explicitly name the remaining boundary (template-bounded queries, no new motor skills, single-image input, sub-90% reliability, label geometry, caption errors). Robotics papers additionally close on ENABLED DOWNSTREAM CAPABILITY or a RELEASED RECIPE (OpenVLA open weights + LoRA; ConceptGraphs as a perception backbone) — the contribution is framed as 'unlocks X' / 'reproducible recipe,' not merely 'metric went up.' Intensifier-light, evidence-anchored register dominates; the one outlier ('consistently and definitively outperform', Diffusion Policy) still ties the adjective to a 12-task sweep.
- SEEN-vs-UNSEEN / GENERALIZATION-AXIS PARTITIONING is the standard threat-to-validity control: evaluation sets are pre-split into in-distribution vs novel-object/background/environment (RT-2, OpenVLA) or held-out-from-training human benchmarks (SpatialVLM, SpatialRGPT) so that 'it generalizes' is tested on data the model provably never saw. Cross-embodiment / cross-platform demonstration (OpenVLA multi-robot; ConceptGraphs Jackal + Spot) substitutes for a held-out test split in robotics, where a fixed test set is impractical.

## Spatial intelligence for autonomous systems: label uncertainty, occlusion-aware QA, calibr

**Papers:**
- GLENet: Boosting 3D Object Detectors with Generative Label Uncertainty Estimation (International Journal of Computer Vision (IJCV) 2023; arXiv:2207.02466)
- Labels Are Not Perfect: Inferring Spatial Uncertainty in Object Detection (arXiv:2012.12195 (2020), extended journal/IROS-lineage version of 'Inferring Spatial Uncertainty in Object Detection' (IROS 2020, arXiv:2003.03644))
- CAPTURe: Evaluating Spatial Reasoning in Vision Language Models via Occluded Object Counting (ICCV 2025; arXiv:2504.15485)
- Beyond the Visible: Benchmarking Occlusion Perception in Multimodal Large Language Models (arXiv:2508.04059 (2025))
- Exploring Aleatoric Uncertainty in Object Detection via Vision Foundation Models (arXiv:2411.17767 (2024, under review))
- A Theoretical and Practical Framework for Evaluating Uncertainty Calibration in Object Detection (arXiv:2309.00464 (submitted 2023, revised 2024))
- ActiveAD: Planning-Oriented Active Learning for End-to-End Autonomous Driving (arXiv:2403.02877 (2024))
- Identifying Driver Interactions via Conditional Behavior Prediction (IEEE ICRA 2021 (Waymo); arXiv:2104.09959)

**Shared patterns:**
- Manufacture the oracle when the ground truth is unobservable. Across the uncertainty/occlusion papers (GLENet, Labels Are Not Perfect, CAPTURe, Beyond the Visible) the 'true' quantity — label variance, hidden-object count, occlusion rate — cannot be directly observed, so each paper GENERATES it: a CVAE over point clouds, a generative LiDAR model, a deterministic pattern-continuation rule, or programmatic compositing whose parameters ARE the labels. Necessity/value is then measured against this constructed oracle. The honesty test is whether they admit the oracle is a proxy (GLENet and Labels-Are-Not-Perfect do; the SAM paper leaves it implicit).
- Prove necessity/value with a counterfactual or auxiliary-information ablation, not just a headline metric. The strongest 'necessity' claims come from removing or supplying the quantity and measuring the change: ActiveAD's component build-up (each valuation term's marginal L2/collision gain), the Waymo paper's agent-removal trajectory shift, and CAPTURe's give-the-model-the-coordinates ablation (8.32 pt sMAPE). 'X matters' is demonstrated as 'remove/add X and the outcome moves by delta.'
- Relative framing dominates; absolute calibration is the exception. Six of eight lead with RELATIVE numbers (AP deltas, budget-vs-performance ratios, occluded-minus-unoccluded gaps, human-vs-model ratios). Only the calibration-framework paper (2309.00464) and the simulation leg of Labels-Are-Not-Perfect commit to ABSOLUTE targets. This mirrors a field norm — beat-the-baseline over measure-the-true-value — and is exactly where calibration work pushes back.
- Almost everything is inductive (benchmark/empirical); the rare deductive cores are the most defensible. The calibration framework (formal definition -> metric) and the Waymo interactivity score (KL from first principles) define their target analytically, then confirm empirically. These hybrids resist the 'SOTA-overfit' critique better than pure benchmark induction, and both pair the definition with a confirmatory perturbation test.
- Success criteria are essentially never pre-registered; targets are stated post-hoc, occasionally up-front-but-informal. None of the eight pre-register. ActiveAD comes closest by stating the 30%-matches-100% target before the experiments; the rest define success as 'beat the baseline on benchmark Y.' The implicit kill condition is uniform and recoverable from each paper: 'if the added/removed quantity did not change the metric, the central claim fails' — but it is rarely written as an explicit falsification clause.
- Occlusion is treated as the canonical hard case and the natural difficulty axis. It is the explicit subject (CAPTURe, Beyond the Visible), the cited source of label ambiguity (GLENet, Labels Are Not Perfect), and the qualitative correlate of high uncertainty (the SAM paper). Yet the empirical occlusion->miscalibration link is repeatedly asserted more strongly than it is quantified — a concrete frontier gap: a paper that stratifies a FORMAL calibration metric (a la 2309.00464) by measured occlusion level, with a stated effect size and kill condition, does not yet exist in this set.
- Conclusions split cleanly into 'instrument' vs 'diagnosis,' and the value claim follows the type. Engineering papers (GLENet, SAM-uncertainty, ActiveAD, Waymo) conclude with a reusable module/score and prove value via downstream gain; benchmark papers (CAPTURe, Beyond the Visible) conclude with infrastructure + a measured deficit and prove necessity via the size of the gap to humans/oracle. A spatial-probe paper should pick its lane: either ship a valuation/uncertainty signal proven by a remove-it ablation, or ship a diagnostic oracle proven by a human/derived-ground-truth gap — and, unlike the surveyed work, write the kill condition and (ideally) pre-register the success threshold explicitly.
