# Stage 6 — vis-calibration: is occupancy-model confidence honest where the sensor cannot see? (DEFINE — last in program order; descriptive direction-of-effect is the load-bearing claim, recalibration is a separately-labeled stretch)

## Motivation
PRECISE GAP: occupancy models emit a dense per-voxel confidence over space the sensor largely never saw — on the Occ3D-nuScenes mini frames I inspected, ~77-80% of voxels are UNOBSERVED (camera 20.4% / lidar 22.7% observed). A planner that trusts a high-confidence 'free' in an unobserved voxel is trusting a learned shape/co-occurrence prior, not a measurement. The field reports calibration (ECE) as a SINGLE whole-grid number that averages observed and unobserved together — which structurally cannot answer 'is the confidence honest precisely where there was no evidence?'. The released Occ3D per-voxel visibility mask is exactly the partition that turns this into a falsifiable test, and it already sits in this repo's adapter (occ3d.py map_occupancy / mask_lidar / mask_camera, present and binary on all 10 mini scenes). No prior work conditions ECE on a released per-voxel visibility mask across ≥2 models with this control battery; ReliOcc supplies the ECE_geo/ECE_sem protocol but (per the verification ledger) its public code is stub-or-unverified, so the construct is reimplemented self-contained. WHY THIS STAGE IS LAST AND CHEAP: it is the highest-dispute-risk, oracle-light topic, deliberately deferred so the shared occquery/gt-distrust visibility-mask plumbing (raycast + Occ3D adapter + mask handling, already built, 76 tests green) makes it nearly free to stand up. The analysis is pure numpy over saved prediction arrays — fully CPU/Mac-feasible — and the ONLY GPU-bound step is obtaining the per-voxel scored predictions once, which is the same honest CUDA boundary PLAN already draws for the v1 predicted-occupancy arm.

## Central hypothesis
CENTRAL (one falsifiable sentence): On released camera-only occupancy models, predictions in voxels the sensor never observed are systematically MORE over-confident than predictions in observed voxels — i.e. expected calibration error in the released-mask unobserved stratum is strictly and significantly worse than in the observed stratum, on the SAME scored voxels, on ≥2 independently-trained checkpoints. ARGUMENT TYPE: this is an INDUCTIVE/DESCRIPTIVE direction-of-effect claim (measure a per-stratum calibration gap, generalize across two models and a second substrate), built on a quasi-DEDUCTIVE scaffold (the model receives NO sensor evidence in unobserved voxels, so its confidence there is a learned shape/co-occurrence prior, not grounded measurement; a prior that is not penalized in the observed-only training loss has no reason to be calibrated). The construct (per-stratum ECE on the released visibility mask) is itself the contribution — it makes the wrong claim ("the model is calibrated") structurally hard to make by refusing to average calibration over a partition the sensor coverage defines. This is the same shape as SparseOcc's RayIoU and the lab's own expressivity witness: reform the MEASUREMENT, do not post an accuracy number. It MEASURES whether confidence is honest per visibility stratum; it does NOT judge whether a mis-calibrated voxel is dangerous (that is the dynfield/occquery×dynfield axis) and does NOT claim the model's POINT prediction is wrong (a confidently-correct unobserved voxel is in scope and must be counted).

## Sub-hypotheses
- H1 (descriptive gap, oracle-free headline, no model-training): Partitioning scored voxels by the RELEASED Occ3D-nuScenes per-voxel visibility mask into observed (mask=1) vs unobserved (mask=0), the unobserved stratum has strictly higher ECE_sem (semantic-class calibration) AND/OR ECE_geo (binary occupied-vs-free calibration) than the observed stratum, with non-overlapping per-scene bootstrap 95% CIs, on BOTH of ≥2 public camera-only occupancy checkpoints. Needs NO oracle beyond the GT label already shipped with Occ3D and the released mask — the headline. EMPIRICALLY GROUNDED: the mask is real and binary {0,1}, ~77-80% of voxels are unobserved on the mini frames I inspected (camera 20.4% / lidar 22.7% observed), so the stratum sizes are non-degenerate.
- H2 (mechanism / not-an-artifact, MECE ablation): The gap survives the three confounds that could fake it — (a) it is NOT explained by the observed/unobserved class-mix difference (re-weight each stratum to a common per-class distribution, or compute per-class ECE then macro-average so 'unobserved is mostly free, free is easy' cannot drive the result); (b) it is NOT explained by distance-from-ego (unobserved voxels are farther; stratify the comparison within matched ego-distance bins so range is held fixed); (c) it is NOT a binning artifact (it holds across equal-width AND equal-mass binning and across bin counts ∈ {10,15,20}, and survives ACE/adaptive ECE). If the gap vanishes under any single matched control, H1 is downgraded to 'a class/range composition effect, not a visibility effect'.
- H3 (substrate transfer, external validity): The DIRECTION of the gap (unobserved worse) reproduces on a second visibility-mask substrate — either the Occ3D-Waymo released mask, or the LiDAR-vs-camera mask split within Occ3D-nuScenes treated as two independent definitions of 'unobserved' — so the effect is a property of unobserved-region prediction, not of one mask's labeling quirk. This directly pre-empts the kill condition 'the gap is an artifact of one custom labeling.'
- H4 (STRETCH, separately labeled, NOT load-bearing, NEEDS GPU): A visibility-aware recalibration (a per-stratum temperature, fit on a held-out scene split using ONLY the released mask as the conditioning feature) reduces the unobserved-stratum ECE gap on a held-out test split without inflating observed-stratum ECE. Any specific '≤X% ECE' target is aspirational until measured and is reported as a curve, never a headline number. This leg is gated on producing scored checkpoints (see kill_criteria / threats) and is explicitly the 'instrument-proven-by-remove-it' lane, kept distinct from the H1 diagnosis lane.

## Success criteria (pre-registered)
- SMOKE (internal, NOT a scientific result, Mac-feasible today): the per-stratum ECE pipeline computes end-to-end on ONE public checkpoint's released predictions — partition by mask, build reliability diagrams, emit ECE_sem + ECE_geo per stratum with per-scene bootstrap CIs — and a unit-tested toy case with a hand-constructed mis-calibrated unobserved stratum returns a gap of the known sign. Reuses the Occ3D adapter's existing visibility-mask path (occ3d.map_occupancy mask!=None already marks mask==0 as UNKNOWN; the calibration code reads the raw mask, not the UNKNOWN-collapsed grid). Mirrors occquery's 'Smoke (done)' tier: code works, NOT evidence.
- PUBLISHABLE (the load-bearing result, DESCRIPTIVE): on ≥2 independently-trained public camera-only occupancy checkpoints evaluated on the Occ3D-nuScenes val split, the unobserved stratum is significantly worse than the observed stratum on BOTH ECE_sem and ECE_geo (per-scene bootstrap 95% CIs non-overlapping on BOTH models), the gap SURVIVES the H2 class-mix + distance + binning controls, and the DIRECTION reproduces on the H3 second substrate. Reported as reliability diagrams + a full ECE-vs-bin-count + ECE-vs-distance-bin curve, NOT a single ECE. The RELATIVE per-stratum gap (unobserved − observed, on the same checkpoint, same val split) is the load-bearing claim; any absolute ECE value is a secondary internal-validity readout and is never compared across checkpoints, modalities, or dataset versions.
- STRONG (only if it lands cleanly): H4 visibility-aware recalibration shrinks the held-out unobserved-stratum gap measurably without hurting the observed stratum, released as code + frozen config + held-out scene IDs so the budget/curve is adoptable as a calibration-audit tool. Explicitly labeled a stretch; its absence does NOT weaken the Publishable diagnosis.
- PRE-REGISTRATION (binding before any number is read): fix BEFORE running — the exact ≥2 checkpoints and their commit/weights hashes; the val scene IDs; ECE definition (equal-mass, 15 bins as primary, with {10,20} + equal-width as the reported sensitivity sweep); the bootstrap protocol (per-scene resample, 1000 draws, 95% percentile CI); the significance rule (non-overlapping per-scene bootstrap CIs on BOTH models, NOT a single p-value); and the matched-control construction for H2. A cutoff a reviewer can move is not a result — every threshold ships as a curve.
- REUSE GATE (consistency with the program): the experiment adds NOTHING to src/probe/ core until a second consumer exists; the per-stratum ECE + reliability-diagram code lives in experiments/vis_calibration/ and only the visibility-mask stratification helper (already shared with gt-distrust) sits in core. Honors the 'extract on second use' invariant.

## Kill / pivot criteria (pre-registered)
- KILL (thesis falsified, honest negative is publishable): on the released mask, observed and unobserved ECE are statistically INDISTINGUISHABLE (per-scene bootstrap CIs overlap) on either model, OR the sign is inconsistent across the ≥2 checkpoints (one model worse unobserved, the other worse observed) — then 'confidence is less honest where the sensor can't see' is false as a general effect and is reported as a measured null, not buried.
- PIVOT-1 (artifact, not visibility): if the gap exists but VANISHES under any single H2 matched control (class-mix re-weighting, OR distance-bin matching, OR a different binning scheme), the effect is a composition/range/binning artifact — re-scope the claim to exactly the surviving factor (e.g. 'far-field semantic ECE is worse, independent of visibility') and drop the visibility framing. Do not present a confound-driven gap as a visibility result.
- PIVOT-2 (labeling-specific): if the gap is present on the Occ3D-nuScenes mask but does NOT reproduce in direction on the H3 second substrate, the effect is a property of one mask's construction, not of unobserved prediction — shrink to a single-dataset descriptive note explicitly labeled non-transferable.
- KILL-FEASIBILITY (the CUDA wall, must be resolved before committing to H1 at scale): the hypothesis requires per-voxel SCORED predictions (class logits + geometric occupancy probability), which a deterministic predicate cannot produce — they come from a trained occupancy model. If NO released checkpoint ships raw per-voxel scores AND none can be obtained without local GPU inference (none is available: arm64 Mac, no CUDA, torch not installed), then H1 cannot be run on a Mac at all. Mitigations in priority order: (i) use checkpoints that release pre-computed per-voxel prediction dumps on val; (ii) borrow a one-off rented/Colab GPU ONLY to dump logits once, then do ALL calibration analysis on CPU on the Mac (the analysis is pure numpy over saved arrays — confirmed feasible); (iii) if neither, the stage stays a written protocol + smoke only and is honestly reported as blocked-on-compute, exactly as PLAN treats the v1 predicted-occupancy arm.
- KILL-H4 (recalibration leg only): if the visibility-aware temperature does not beat a global temperature on the held-out unobserved stratum, drop H4 — it does not weaken the H1 diagnosis, which is the actual paper.

## Oracle
GROUND TRUTH IS SHIPPED, NOT RECONSTRUCTED — this is the cleanest oracle situation in the whole program. Calibration needs three inputs and all are released, so there is NO author-built oracle and NO self-oracle to defend: (1) the per-voxel CORRECTNESS label = the Occ3D-nuScenes dense GT semantics (the same field occquery's adapter already loads via map_occupancy(mask=None)); a prediction is 'correct' iff its argmax class matches GT (for ECE_sem) or its occupied/free call matches GT occupancy (for ECE_geo). (2) The per-voxel CONFIDENCE = the released checkpoint's own softmax/sigmoid score (the model's claim about itself — nothing to construct). (3) The PARTITION variable = the released Occ3D per-voxel visibility mask (verified present, binary {0,1}, on all 10 mini scenes; the exact mask used by gt-distrust/occquery plumbing). ECE then bins predictions by confidence and compares mean-confidence to empirical-accuracy per bin — a standard, fully-specified construct (reimplement ReliOcc's ECE_geo/ECE_sem if its code is unreleased; ECE itself is textbook). AVOIDING THE CIRCULAR ORACLE (the lab's hardest rule): the calibration GT (Occ3D semantics) must NOT be derived from, or share a data path with, the model whose confidence is being judged — so checkpoints trained WITH Occ3D-nuScenes GT as supervision are evaluated against that same GT, which is the standard held-out-val setup (the model never saw VAL voxels' labels at train time), and the visibility mask is from the SENSOR/dataset, not from the model's own raycast (unlike gt-distrust's dropped camera-mask leg, here the mask never touches the model). The one genuine circularity to refuse: do NOT define 'unobserved' using the model's own uncertainty (that would make the partition a function of the thing measured) — the partition is the EXTERNAL released mask only. RELEASE: the ECE/stratification code + the exact val scene IDs + the checkpoint hashes, so anyone recomputes the gap. SYNTHETIC vs REAL: a synthetic mis-calibrated grid is for unit-testing the ECE math (smoke) ONLY; it is never evidence — the result requires real released checkpoints on the real val mask, per 'synthetic results are not scientific results.'

## Baselines
- NAIVE / NO-PARTITION baseline (the 'box-only' analogue for this stage): the single whole-grid ECE the field normally reports, with NO visibility stratification. The entire contribution is that this number HIDES the effect — showing whole-grid ECE looks fine while the unobserved stratum is badly mis-calibrated is the stage's reason to exist (mirrors how occquery's expressivity gap shows the box-only language cannot even pose the query).
- MATCHED-CONTROL baselines for H2 (the MECE confound controls, each isolating ONE alternative explanation): (a) class-mix-matched ECE (re-weight strata to a common per-class distribution / per-class macro-ECE) — rules out 'unobserved is mostly easy free space'; (b) distance-from-ego-matched ECE (compare strata only within equal ego-range bins) — rules out 'unobserved just means far'; (c) random-partition control — partition voxels into two random halves of the SAME sizes as observed/unobserved and confirm the ECE gap is ~0, proving the real gap is the mask and not the split sizes. The random-partition control is the calibration analogue of gt-distrust's occlusion-depth-shuffled control.
- TEMPERATURE / RECALIBRATION baselines for H4 (only if H4 is run): global temperature scaling and Platt/vector scaling fit IGNORING visibility — the visibility-aware recalibration must beat these on the held-out unobserved stratum to earn the 'Strong' claim, else H4 is dropped.
- MODEL-DIVERSITY as a baseline-of-models: ≥2 INDEPENDENTLY-trained camera-only occupancy checkpoints (different architectures/training runs) are required so the effect is not one model's quirk — the analogue of RefAV's Claude/Gemini/Qwen swap. PROVISIONAL until checkpoint availability + raw-score release is primary-confirmed; if a public checkpoint is a stub or ships no per-voxel scores, it is not a usable baseline (the 'stub baselines are not baselines' rule) and is replaced by another or by a one-off GPU logit-dump.

## Ablations
- Visibility partition vs NO partition (whole-grid ECE) — the headline ablation: shows the single number the field reports HIDES the unobserved-stratum effect. Maps to H1.
- Class-mix-matched vs raw per-stratum ECE (re-weight to common per-class distribution, or per-class macro-ECE) — isolates 'is it visibility or is it the unobserved-is-mostly-free class skew'. THE most important ablation (the verified FREE-dominance of the unobserved stratum makes this the likeliest confound). Maps to H2(a).
- Distance-matched vs raw per-stratum ECE (compare strata only within equal ego-range bins) — isolates visibility from range, since unobserved voxels are systematically farther. Maps to H2(b).
- Binning sweep: equal-width vs equal-mass × {10,15,20} bins, plus adaptive/ACE — isolates a binning artifact from a real effect. Maps to H2(c).
- Random-partition control (two random halves of the SAME stratum sizes) — must yield ~0 gap; proves the effect is the MASK, not the split sizes. The calibration analogue of gt-distrust's shuffled-occlusion control.
- ECE_geo vs ECE_sem (binary occupancy-confidence vs semantic-class-confidence) — separates 'is space occupied' calibration from 'what class' calibration; the effect may live in one and not the other, and that is a finding, not a failure.
- Model-swap: checkpoint A vs checkpoint B (≥2 independent architectures/runs) — the RefAV-style diversity ablation; the gap's sign must agree across both. Maps to H1/H3.
- Mask-source swap: lidar-mask vs camera-mask definition of 'unobserved' within Occ3D-nuScenes (verified both present, both binary) — a cheap first cut at H3 substrate-transfer before bringing in a second dataset.
- H4 only (stretch): visibility-aware temperature vs global temperature vs no recalibration, on a held-out scene split — the remove-it ablation that proves the diagnosis is actionable.

## Metrics
PRIMARY (load-bearing, RELATIVE): the per-stratum ECE GAP = ECE(unobserved) − ECE(observed), computed on the SAME checkpoint, SAME val split, for BOTH ECE_geo (binary occupied-vs-free confidence calibration) and ECE_sem (semantic-class confidence calibration), with per-scene bootstrap 95% CIs on the gap itself. The gap is the claim; its sign and CI-separation are what success/kill hinge on. SECONDARY (internal-validity only, NEVER cross-protocol-compared): the absolute per-stratum ECE values, mean-confidence and empirical-accuracy per reliability bin. REPORTED-AS-CURVES (no movable single number): ECE-gap vs bin-count {10,15,20} × {equal-width, equal-mass}; ECE-gap vs ego-distance bin (this doubles as the H2 distance control); per-class ECE bars (the H2 class-mix control); reliability diagrams per stratum per model. CONTROL METRICS: the random-partition ECE gap (must be ≈0 within CI), the class-mix-matched gap, the distance-matched gap — H1 only stands if the real gap survives all three. H4 (stretch): held-out unobserved-stratum ECE before vs after visibility-aware temperature, vs global-temperature, as a curve over the temperature/feature budget. RELATIVE-vs-ABSOLUTE FRAMING (explicit, per the cross-cutting rule): absolute ECE is modality- and checkpoint-dependent and is NEVER compared across the two checkpoints or against any published ECE (different model/version/supervision = 'adjacent, not comparable'); only the within-checkpoint observed-vs-unobserved RELATIVE gap carries the result, and the secondary checkpoint exists to show the gap's DIRECTION replicates, not to compare magnitudes.

## Expected results
EXPECTED (if the hypothesis holds): on both checkpoints, the unobserved-stratum ECE (both _geo and _sem) is materially and significantly higher than observed (per-scene bootstrap CIs non-overlapping), driven by over-confidence (mean confidence > empirical accuracy) concentrated in the high-confidence bins of the unobserved stratum — visible as a reliability diagram bowing below the diagonal for unobserved while observed tracks the diagonal. The gap SURVIVES class-mix matching (it is not just 'unobserved = easy free space'), survives distance matching (it is not just 'far'), is stable in sign across bin schemes, and reproduces in DIRECTION on the 2nd substrate. The random-partition control gap is ≈0. EXACTLY WHAT FALSIFIES THIS (pre-stated, the kill surface): (a) overlapping per-scene bootstrap CIs between strata on either model → null, hypothesis false, reported as a measured null; (b) opposite sign across the two checkpoints → not a general effect, killed; (c) the gap present whole-grid but ERASED by the class-mix OR distance OR binning control → it was a composition/range/binning artifact, re-scoped to the surviving factor and the visibility framing dropped; (d) gap present on Occ3D-nuScenes but absent in direction on the 2nd substrate → labeling-specific, shrunk to a single-dataset note; (e) the random-partition control itself shows a non-zero gap → the pipeline is broken (split-size confound), fix before any claim. HONEST PRIOR: the deductive scaffold predicts a real gap, but the class-mix confound is the most likely way a naive version of this fakes a result (unobserved voxels are dominated by FREE id-17, which is the easiest class — I verified ~493k of ~495k unobserved voxels in scene-0061 f0 are free), so H2's class-mix control is the single most important guard and is where this experiment most plausibly dies or downgrades.

## Scope — in
- IN: the descriptive per-stratum calibration GAP (unobserved − observed ECE) on released camera-only occupancy checkpoints, on the released Occ3D visibility mask — measured, with CIs, reproduced across ≥2 models and a 2nd substrate.
- IN: ECE_geo (binary occupied/free confidence) AND ECE_sem (semantic-class confidence) as two distinct calibration readouts, each with reliability diagrams + bin/distance sensitivity curves.
- IN: the MECE controls that establish the gap is a VISIBILITY effect, not a class-mix / distance / binning artifact.
- IN (stretch, separately labeled): a visibility-aware recalibration that shrinks the held-out gap — proving the diagnosis is actionable.
- IN: full release of stratification + ECE code, val scene IDs, and checkpoint hashes for external recompute. The contribution is the per-visibility-stratum calibration construct + the measured effect, released as an audit tool.

## Scope — out (what it does NOT claim)
- NOT asserted: that a mis-calibrated unobserved voxel is DANGEROUS or planning-relevant — calibration honesty is a property of the model's confidence, not a danger verdict; danger requires relative motion over time = the dynfield axis (the same MEASURES-not-JUDGES fence occquery draws).
- NOT asserted: that the model's POINT prediction in unobserved regions is WRONG — a confidently-correct hallucinated-but-right voxel is in scope and counted; the claim is about confidence HONESTY (calibration), not accuracy. Reporting accuracy as if it were calibration would be the category error the lab already caught once.
- NOT asserted: a universal effect-size ('unobserved ECE is X% worse everywhere') — the magnitude is model- and dataset-specific and never compared cross-protocol; only the within-checkpoint relative direction/gap is load-bearing.
- NOT asserted: that visibility-aware recalibration is a deployable safety method — H4 is a held-out feasibility demonstration of the diagnosis being actionable, not a certified planner-side guarantee.
- NOT asserted: any claim derived from a single ECE number, a single bin count, a single mask, or a single model — each of those is pre-registered as a sensitivity axis, not a result.
- OUT of this stage entirely: training a new occupancy model, or claiming a leaderboard placement — this is DEFINE (no leaderboard); the bar is public data + a measurable gap vs the no-partition baseline + a released benchmark, NOT ≥SOTA.

## Threats to validity
- CIRCULAR ORACLE (the lab's hardest trap): if 'unobserved' were defined from the model's own uncertainty, the partition would be a function of the measured quantity. NEUTRALIZED: the partition is the EXTERNAL released sensor/dataset mask only, never the model's output; the correctness GT (Occ3D semantics) is dataset-derived and was held out of the checkpoint's training labels for val voxels. This is strictly cleaner than gt-distrust (whose camera-mask leg was dropped for sharing the flag's own raycast) — here the mask never touches the model.
- CLASS-MIX CONFOUND (most likely failure mode): the unobserved stratum is dominated by FREE (verified ~493k/495k free voxels in one inspected frame), the easiest class, so a raw ECE gap could be pure composition. NEUTRALIZED by the class-mix-matched / per-class macro-ECE ablation (H2a); if the gap dies here it is honestly downgraded.
- DISTANCE CONFOUND: unobserved ⊃ far-field, and far-field is harder regardless of visibility. NEUTRALIZED by distance-bin matching (H2b), which separates 'unseen' from 'far'.
- BINNING / ESTIMATOR ARTIFACT: ECE is bin-sensitive and biased. NEUTRALIZED by the equal-width/equal-mass × {10,15,20} + adaptive sweep, reporting the gap as a curve; no single bin count is trusted.
- STUB / UNAVAILABLE CHECKPOINTS (the 'stub baselines are not baselines' rule): if a public camera-only checkpoint ships no per-voxel scores or its repo is a stub, it is not usable; mitigated by requiring ≥2 score-releasing checkpoints or a one-off GPU logit-dump, and by reimplementing ReliOcc's ECE self-contained (its code is stub-or-unverified per the ledger).
- CROSS-PROTOCOL COMPARISON (forbidden): absolute ECE across the two checkpoints or vs any published ECE differs in model/version/supervision/modality and is NOT comparable. NEUTRALIZED by making ONLY the within-checkpoint relative gap load-bearing; the 2nd checkpoint tests direction-replication, not magnitude.
- MASK SEMANTICS: the released mask is a sensor-COVERAGE definition of 'unobserved' (a binary {0,1} I verified), not a true information-theoretic one; the visible boundary is itself fuzzy. Named as a STRUCTURAL limitation (the only failure surface), and probed by the lidar-vs-camera mask swap + the H3 second substrate; the claim is fenced to 'unobserved as the released mask defines it.'
- CUDA / REPRODUCIBILITY-ON-MAC: no local GPU means predictions must be sourced or dumped once externally; the analysis itself is CPU-reproducible. Versions pinned + every prediction-dump and labels.npz sha256-checksummed (per m2-adapter-contract) so a silently-changed input is caught — a known reproducibility footgun.
- SCOPE-CREEP into danger/accuracy: the standing risk of re-reading a calibration gap as a danger or accuracy claim (the category error the lab caught in occquery). NEUTRALIZED by the explicit MEASURES-not-JUDGES fence and by counting confidently-correct unobserved voxels as in-scope.
- DEGENERATE STRATUM: if a checkpoint only scores observed voxels (some models predict only the visible frustum), the unobserved stratum is empty and the test is undefined for that model — checked first; such a checkpoint is excluded with a stated reason, not silently dropped.

## Argument chain
INDUCTIVE-WITH-DEDUCTIVE-SCAFFOLD, stated in the lab's required form. (1) DEDUCTIVE scaffold (the 'why it should be true' that licenses a causal reading before the test, in the Why-Far-Looks-Up style): in an unobserved voxel the model integrates ZERO sensor evidence, so its confidence there is a function of learned priors only; the standard occupancy training loss is computed on labeled (largely observed) voxels, so there is no gradient pressure for the unobserved-region confidence to be calibrated — therefore over-confidence in the unobserved stratum is the predicted default, and a NULL gap would be the surprising outcome needing explanation. (2) INDUCTIVE core: I cannot prove this for all models, so I MEASURE the per-stratum ECE gap (H1), and to license attributing it to VISIBILITY rather than to a confound I run the remove-the-cause controls (H2: hold class-mix fixed, hold distance fixed, vary binning; the random-partition control removes the 'split-size' explanation, the matched bins remove 'far ≠ unseen'). (3) GENERALIZATION is earned, not assumed: ≥2 independent checkpoints (the effect is not one model's quirk) + a 2nd mask substrate (H3, the effect is not one labeling's quirk). (4) The construct itself is the deductive guard against the field's habitual error: by refusing to average ECE over the visibility partition, the measurement makes the false claim ('the model is calibrated') structurally hard to state — the band-aid-vs-root-cause move applied to a metric. FALLACIES REFUSED BY CONSTRUCTION: affirming-the-consequent (a gap alone is necessary not sufficient → the H2 controls + the matched-random partition supply the counterfactual that removes the claimed cause); circular oracle (the partition is the EXTERNAL released mask, never the model's own uncertainty, and the GT is sensor/dataset-derived, never the model's output); synthetic-to-real (synthetic grids unit-test the ECE math only; the result requires real checkpoints on real val); correlation-as-causation (the deductive no-evidence-in-unobserved scaffold + the distance-matched control separate 'unseen' from 'far'). CONCLUSION FORM (pick one lane, per the limitations rule): the PRIMARY contribution is DIAGNOSIS — a released per-visibility-stratum calibration construct proven by the size of the measured gap vs the no-partition baseline and its survival of controls; H4 (recalibration) is the SEPARATE instrument-proven-by-remove-it lane and is labeled as such so the conclusion RESOLVES the hypothesis (confirmed / null / artifact-scoped), it does not merely announce a benchmark. STRUCTURAL-LIMITATION fence (not a softener): the claim is bounded by what the released mask calls 'unobserved' (a sensor-coverage definition, not a true information-theoretic one) and by the released checkpoints' score semantics (softmax temperature is architecture-specific) — these are named as the only failure surface, exactly as occquery names its 0.4 m voxel-resolution ceiling.

## Adversarial review (2026-06-21) — defects + fixes
### contradiction (harsh top-venue reviewer) lens — verdict: reject-as-unsound (as written). The central construct is fatally undermined by a verified property of the substrate the plan itself relies on: the Occ3D GT carries no independent correctness signal in the lidar-unobserved stratum (it is default-fill free), so per-stratum ECE there is calibration against a near-constant label — the exact circular-oracle the plan claims to have escaped. Plus the plan's own load-bearing empirical numbers do not reproduce. A genuinely sound version may exist (camera-mask stratum + ECE_geo-only, or treat both strata's labels as model-derived and reframe the contribution), but that is a different, much smaller paper. Resubmit only after the oracle problem is resolved with code+data, not prose.
- **blocker**: FATAL ORACLE CIRCULARITY (the plan's headline claim collapses). The plan asserts (oracle section) this is 'the cleanest oracle situation in the whole program' because the correctness GT (Occ3D semantics) is 'dataset-derived, never the model's output.' I verified on the real mini data this is false in the one place that matters — the unobserved stratum. Occ3D GT is itself built by accumulating LiDAR returns + camera-ray masking; where the LiDAR never integrated a return, the GT label is default-fill. Measured on 404 real frames: among lidar-unobserved voxels the non-free GT fraction is 0.0003% (682 of 218,114,179); among voxels unobserved by BOTH sensors in scene-0061 f0 it is exactly 0 non-free (GT hist {11:646,12:6,13:141,14:168,17:565410} — all free/ground). So 'is the model's confidence honest in the unobserved stratum' is being scored against a label that is a near-constant FREE, which is itself the sensor-coverage prior. The model's unobserved-region confidence and the 'GT' it is graded on are BOTH functions of where the sensor didn't see — that is precisely the circular oracle the plan says it refuses. ECE_sem is undefined-in-spirit here (you cannot measure semantic-class calibration where every cell's 'truth' is the same default class), and ECE_geo degenerates (occupied prior = 0.0003%, so every confidence bin maps to ~100% free 'accuracy' and the reliability diagram is trivially near-perfect or trivially over-confident with no information content).
  - fix: Resolve with code+data BEFORE any claim, not prose. Three honest options: (a) RESTRICT the unobserved stratum to camera-unobserved-but-LiDAR-OBSERVED voxels, where the GT is a real measurement (this is the only region with independent ground truth AND no sensor evidence in the camera modality) — but verify it is non-empty per checkpoint (in this mini frame camera-only-minus-lidar = 0 voxels, so this may not even exist at the needed scale); (b) DROP the GT-correctness oracle entirely and reframe the contribution as 'predicted-occupancy self-consistency / sharpness in unobserved space' (an entropy/confidence-distribution claim that needs no correctness label), explicitly NOT a calibration claim; (c) if neither holds, KILL the stage and report the oracle-degeneracy as the finding. The plan must add a pre-registration gate: 'compute non-free GT fraction per stratum per checkpoint; if the unobserved-stratum minority-class fraction < X%, the stratum is calibration-degenerate and the test is void' — this is the missing DEGENERATE-STRATUM guard, which the plan only wrote for empty strata, not for label-constant strata.

  - fix: Re-derive every cited statistic from the data with a committed script and check it in (the repo already mandates sha256-checksummed inputs per m2-adapter-contract). Replace the 20.4/22.7 and 493k/495k figures with the reproduced numbers, cite the exact frame token and scene, and note that these are mini-split numbers that may differ on val. If the author cannot reproduce their own cited numbers, treat the pre-registration as not yet done.
- **blocker**: H2 CLASS-MIX CONTROL IS NOT A CONTROL — IT IS THE WHOLE EFFECT, AND IT IS UNCORRECTABLE HERE. The plan calls the class-mix-matched ECE 'THE most important ablation' and concedes 'this is where this experiment most plausibly dies.' The data shows it does not just plausibly die — it is structurally dead. The unobserved-by-lidar stratum is 99.9997% free; the observed (camera) stratum is 11.18% non-free. You cannot 're-weight each stratum to a common per-class distribution' when one stratum has essentially zero mass in every non-free class (682 non-free voxels total across 218M).   - fix: Delete the 'strictly cleaner than gt-distrust' claim. - **major**: DEDUCTIVE SCAFFOLD CONTRADICTS THE MEASUREMENT IT LICENSES. The argument_chain's deductive scaffold says the model 'integrates ZERO sensor evidence' in unobserved voxels so its confidence is 'a learned prior with no gradient pressure to be calibrated' — predicting over-confidence. But the SAME structural fact makes the GT in those voxels a learned/default prior too (blocker 1). The scaffold therefore predicts a HIGH-accuracy, high-confidence unobserved stratum (good calibration), not a mis-calibrated one — the opposite of the central hypothesis. The deductive story and the empirical substrate point in opposite directions.
  - fix: Reconcile the scaffold with the data: if unobserved GT is ~100% free and the model predicts ~free with high confidence, the expected result is LOW ECE_geo in the unobserved stratum (well-calibrated on the easy default), which would falsify the central hypothesis via the class-mix mechanism. State this as the most likely outcome and pre-register it as a KILL, rather than presenting over-confidence as the default expectation.
- **major**: ECE_geo AND ECE_sem ARE BOTH CLAIMED AS HEADLINE READOUTS, BUT NEITHER IS WELL-POSED IN THE UNOBSERVED STRATUM, YET success_criteria PUBLISHABLE REQUIRES 'significantly worse on BOTH.' Given occupied-prior ≈ 0.0003% (ECE_geo) and a constant-class label (ECE_sem) in the unobserved-by-lidar stratum, requiring a significant gap on BOTH metrics is a criterion that the substrate cannot satisfy honestly — any 'significant' ECE_geo gap there will be an artifact of the degenerate base rate, and any ECE_sem number is meaningless. The success bar is thus internally inconsistent with the measured data.
  - fix: Drop the 'BOTH metrics' requirement. If the stage survives at all, it survives on exactly one well-posed metric in exactly one well-posed stratum-pair, and that must be demonstrated to be non-degenerate (base-rate and label-entropy reported) before it is allowed to be a success criterion.

The provenance of the shared helper is asserted, not shown.
  - fix: State explicitly that the stratification helper is introduced by gt-distrust (first use) and extracted to core only when vis-calibration consumes it (second use), and confirm gt-distrust actually creates it. If gt-distrust does not, this stage cannot rely on a pre-existing shared helper.

### MECE adversarial review, top-venue reviewer. Ground-truthed against the repo: occ3d.py mask path confirmed (map_occupancy marks mask==0 UNKNOWN, raw mask readable), 76 tests collected, camera 20.4 and lidar 22.7 percent observed on scene-0061 frame 0. Measured non-free support across all 10 mini frames: lidar-unobserved 2.43 percent non-free (13026 occupied voxels total), camera-unobserved 2.31 percent. The plan cites FREE-dominance for one frame only and never propagates it into the hypothesis structure. lens — verdict: revise. The core construct (per-visibility-stratum ECE on the released mask, within-checkpoint relative gap as the claim, whole-grid ECE as the foil) is sound and well-fenced against the circular-oracle and synthetic-to-real traps, and the H2 confound battery (class, distance, binning, plus random-partition) is genuinely MECE for the three confounds it names. It is not accept-ready. Two blockers must be fixed first: (1) scored voxels is not a defined partition once model coverage differs from mask coverage, so the strata are not collectively exhaustive across the required 2-plus models; (2) ECE_sem is near-undefined in the unobserved stratum (lidar-unobserved 2.43 percent non-free, about 13k occupied voxels over 10 frames), so the ECE_sem-with-ECE_geo headline and the H2a per-class control are under-powered to inconclusiveness, which the FREE-dominated 77-80 percent framing hides. The four majors (H1-H2 evidence double-counting, H4 split leakage, KILL-vs-PIVOT-1 overlap, and the unmodeled GT-noise-vs-dishonesty confound) are each closable by tightening pre-registration, not redesign. Fix the two blockers (define V_scored as a strict partition with an exclusion gate, and make ECE_geo the sole load-bearing readout while power-gating unobserved ECE_sem) and the plan reaches accept-grade. The thesis is not unsound, it is under-specified at exactly the two points where the measured data says it will break.

Dense-grid models score every voxel; frustum-or-BEV models score only the visible frustum, so the mask-unobserved-frustum-only cell has no score and vanishes for some checkpoints. The strata are therefore not collectively exhaustive over the same voxel set across the required 2-plus models.
  - fix: Pre-register V_scored as voxels with a model score AND a GT label AND a defined mask bit, partitioned by mask into exactly observed and unobserved, no third bucket. Report per-checkpoint observed-and-unobserved counts and promote the degenerate-stratum threat to a hard exclusion gate in success_criteria.
- **blocker**: ECE_sem is near-undefined in the unobserved stratum and the H2a per-class macro-ECE control is not estimable. Over 10-plus classes that is single-to-double-digit voxels per class per scene, so bootstrap CIs are enormous, yet the AND-or wording lets ECE_sem ride co-equal with ECE_geo.
  - fix: Make ECE_geo the sole load-bearing primary. Report unobserved-stratum ECE_sem only if a pre-registered per-class support N_min is met per checkpoint, else declare under-powered and exclude. Add a pre-registration line with measured occupied support per stratum and a power-or-MDE calc.
- **major**: H1 and H2 are not mutually exclusive as evidence, so success_criteria double-counts. The H2b distance control IS the ECE-gap-vs-distance curve and H2a IS the per-class bars, all from the same arrays and bootstrap as H1; the random-partition control is assigned to both H2 and H1 control metrics. So survives-controls belongs to both, and the KILL-vs-PIVOT-1 fork is ambiguous since a control failure can route to either branch.
  - fix: Strict decision tree, one owner per node: H1 owns existence-sign-cross-model-consistency of the raw gap, H2 owns each control as a separate pass-fail. Route gap-absent-or-sign-inconsistent to KILL, gap-erased-by-a-control to PIVOT-1, all-controls-pass-but-H3-fails to PIVOT-2. Remove survives-controls from H1 success.
- **major**: H4 train-and-test split is under-specified and overlaps the H1 eval set, risking leakage. H4 fits a per-stratum temperature on a held-out split and reports on a held-out test split but never states the H1 val IDs and the H4 fit-and-test IDs are disjoint; both draw from the same val split, so the temperature is tuned on the headline-gap scenes. Pre-registration lists val IDs once, not three sets.
  - fix: Pre-register three disjoint scene-ID sets H1-eval, H4-fit, H4-test, with H4-test disjoint from both others, and compute the H1 headline only on H1-eval. Add to the binding pre-registration list.
- **major**: KILL and PIVOT-1 overlap on the cross-model sign-inconsistency case with no precedence. Model A gap can be confound-driven (vanishes under class-mix matching) while model B is real, giving sign-inconsistency that is actually a one-model artifact, not a true null. The plan routes this to KILL when the correct reading is PIVOT-1.
  - fix: Run the H2 controls on each checkpoint independently BEFORE adjudicating cross-model sign consistency, and compare signs only of the control-surviving gaps. KILL equals control-surviving gaps sign-inconsistent or null; PIVOT-1 equals a gap fails a control on one-or-more models.
- **minor**: H3 treats Occ3D-Waymo and the intra-nuScenes lidar-vs-camera split as interchangeable, but the latter shares sensors and scenes (the two masks are about 97-99.8 percent FREE in their unobserved sets, highly correlated) and so cannot rule out the PIVOT-2 labeling-quirk failure it is meant to pre-empt.
  - fix: Require Occ3D-Waymo or another independent dataset as the binding H3; demote the lidar-vs-camera swap to an intra-dataset robustness check that does not clear PIVOT-2. Pre-register which substrate is binding.
- **minor**: The random-partition control target (gap approximately zero within CI) has no pre-registered tolerance, so it cannot fail cleanly. ECE is a biased estimator and a finite-sample random split of unequal 20-80 halves generically shows a small non-zero ECE difference from bin-occupancy alone, making broken-vs-fine a post-hoc call.
  - fix: Pre-register the tolerance as the bootstrap null band of the random-split gap: run K random splits at the true 20-80 sizes and require the real mask gap to exceed that band, making it a proper permutation test.
- **minor**: ECE_geo correctness depends on the adapter FREE-or-OCCUPIED mapping, which is non-trivial: occ3d.py folds class 17 plus ground classes 11, 12, 13, 14 into FREE. A checkpoint geometric-occupancy head may use a different boundary or a dedicated logit, so re-deriving occupied-or-free from argmax injects a silent mapping mismatch into the calibrated quantity.
  - fix: Pre-register the exact occupied-or-free derivation for both the adapter GT mapping and the model head-or-threshold, verify the same class-to-binary map per checkpoint, prefer a native geometric-occupancy probability over argmax, and sha256 the prediction dump (m2-adapter-contract checksums only labels.npz today).
- **minor**: The GT-label-noise vs model-dishonesty confound shared with gt-distrust is unfenced. scope_out fences danger (dynfield) but not the GT-trustworthiness axis. If unobserved-stratum GT is noisier (the gt-distrust thesis: labels through an occluder are untrustworthy), the ECE gap partly reflects GT noise, not over-confidence, and the class-distance-binning battery does not cover this.
  - fix: Add a scope_out line fencing GT-label-noise as the gt-distrust axis, and add a sensitivity arm restricting unobserved ECE to voxels gt-distrust flags trustworthy (or report the gap with and without occlusion-flagged voxels).

### logic (inductive/deductive validity) — adversarial top-venue review lens — verdict: revise — the core measurement is real and the empirical premises check out (I verified the masks are binary {0,1}, observed fractions 20.38%/22.66% match the plan, FREE=class-17 dominates the unobserved stratum at 96.9% = 493,795 voxels, matching the plan's '~493k/495k'), and the descriptive 'whole-grid ECE hides the per-stratum gap' construct is a legitimate contribution. - **blocker**: CIRCULAR-ORACLE AT THE GT-CONSTRUCTION LEVEL (not the partition level the plan defends). The plan loudly inoculates against one circularity (defining 'unobserved' from the model's own uncertainty) and declares this 'the cleanest oracle situation in the whole program.' But it never addresses the circularity that actually bites: the correctness GT for the UNOBSERVED stratum is the Occ3D *dense accumulated* multi-sweep label, and the camera-only checkpoints under test were TRAINED on that same Occ3D dense GT — including supervision on the very voxels their own camera frustum never saw that frame. So in the unobserved stratum the model is graded for confidence-honesty against a target it was explicitly trained to reproduce. 'Over-confident where the sensor can't see' and 'confidently reproduces its multi-sweep training target where the sensor can't see' are the same observation under two descriptions; the design cannot separate them. This is affirming-the-consequent: H1-confirmed is consistent with the deductive scaffold ('no sensor evidence ⇒ uncalibrated prior') but EQUALLY consistent with its negation ('the prior is well-supervised by accumulated GT and the model has simply learned it'). The repo's own adapter docstring (src/probe/adapters/occ3d.py lines 48-56) already warns that masking the dense GT 'makes the denotation depend almost entirely on the unknown policy' and that this masked view 'is NOT the occquery oracle' — the plan cites the same adapter as proof of a clean oracle while ignoring the warning attached to it.
  - fix: Demote the headline from a causal 'sensor can't see ⇒ dishonest' reading to a strictly descriptive 'confidence in mask=0 voxels is worse-calibrated against accumulated GT than in mask=1 voxels,' AND add a discriminating test the current design lacks: split the unobserved stratum into (a) never-observed-in-any-sweep voxels vs (b) observed-in-other-sweeps-but-not-this-frame voxels. The deductive scaffold predicts the gap concentrates in (a); the train-target-leakage confound predicts it tracks (b). Without this split the central claim is not falsifiable against its leading alternative.
- **blocker**: DEDUCTIVE SCAFFOLD IS NON-SEQUITUR / OVERSTATED AS 'QUASI-DEDUCTIVE.' The plan repeatedly leans on: 'the observed-only training loss has no gradient pressure for unobserved-region confidence to be calibrated ⇒ over-confidence is the predicted default, a NULL gap would be surprising.' Two of the three premises are asserted, not established, and one is contradicted by the plan's own data. (i) 'Trained observed-only' is assumed but UNVERIFIED for the chosen checkpoints — Occ3D-nuScenes camera methods (BEVFormer/FB-OCC family) are standardly supervised on the FULL dense grid including mask=0 voxels (camera_mask is an optional eval-time filter, not necessarily a loss mask), which would invert the premise. (ii) 'No gradient pressure ⇒ uncalibrated' is itself a non-sequitur: a class-balanced or focal loss over the full grid produces a strong, often well-calibrated free-space prior precisely because free dominates — the plan's own numbers (unobserved is 96.9% free, the single easiest-to-calibrate outcome) make a NULL or even REVERSED gap on ECE_geo a live possibility, not a surprise needing explanation. The plan asserts the opposite ('NULL gap would be the surprising outcome') with no support. An argument that labels its conclusion's negation 'surprising' while the evidence makes that negation likely is rhetorically loading the inductive prior.
  - fix: Either (a) drop the deductive/causal framing entirely and run this as a pure descriptive measurement (the plan half-commits to this but keeps invoking the scaffold to license a causal reading), or (b) VERIFY the loss-masking of each chosen checkpoint from its training code and report it as a pre-registered fact; if any checkpoint supervises the full grid, state plainly that the scaffold does not apply to it and the claim is descriptive-only for that model. Remove the unsupported 'a NULL gap would be surprising' sentence — given 96.9% free in the unobserved stratum, a small/null ECE_geo gap is a perfectly ordinary outcome.
- **major**: H2(a) CLASS-MIX CONTROL IS UNDER-POWERED TO THE POINT OF BEING POTENTIALLY UNFALSIFIABLE FOR ECE_sem, and the plan presents it as the decisive guard while never checking its statistical feasibility. The plan itself names class-mix as 'THE most important ablation' and 'where this experiment most plausibly dies.' I verified the problem it glosses: per FRAME the non-free unobserved voxels are vanishingly thin (scene-0061 f0: class 0 = 5 voxels, class 2 = 15, class 8 = 15, class 12 = 24; only 3.09% of the unobserved stratum is non-free at all). The control cannot distinguish 'no visibility effect' from 'not enough non-free unobserved voxels per scene to measure one.' Aggregating over all 404 frames recovers usable counts, but that breaks the per-scene resampling unit the significance rule depends on. The plan never reconciles 'per-scene bootstrap' with 'per-class matched ECE_sem' — they are in tension.
  - fix: Pre-register a minimum effective sample size per (class × stratum × scene) cell below which ECE_sem is declared UNESTIMABLE for that cell, and specify the estimator behavior (drop-and-report, or pool classes) BEFORE running — otherwise the result is investigator-degrees-of-freedom. Add a power analysis on the mini data (already in-repo) showing the per-scene class-mix-matched ECE_sem gap CI width under H0; if it cannot resolve a plausible effect, EITHER restrict the load-bearing ECE_sem claim to ECE_geo + a pooled (non-per-scene) class-mix check, OR change the resampling unit (e.g. cluster bootstrap over frames with scene-level blocking) and state the autocorrelation caveat. (i) Non-overlap of two separate 95% CIs is a well-known overly-CONSERVATIVE and INCORRECT test of a difference (the correct object is a CI on the GAP itself, which the metrics section does define elsewhere — so the plan contradicts itself: success_criteria/kill use 'non-overlapping per-stratum CIs' while metrics use 'CI on the gap'). Report effective n (scenes) for every CI including the H3 sub-splits and per-class controls. - **major**: H3 'SECOND SUBSTRATE' DOES NOT PROVIDE THE INDEPENDENCE IT CLAIMS — the cheap version is circular and the strong version is unbudgeted, so the generalization leg is structurally weak. The plan offers two H3 substrates: (a) Occ3D-Waymo released mask, (b) 'lidar-vs-camera mask split within Occ3D-nuScenes treated as two independent definitions of unobserved.' Option (b) is not independent: I verified lidar and camera masks are produced from the SAME Occ3D pipeline on the SAME scenes and are highly correlated (both ~20-23% observed, derived from overlapping sensor coverage of one rig); 'reproduces on the lidar mask too' is close to 'reproduces when I perturb the mask slightly,' which does NOT pre-empt the stated kill condition ('artifact of one custom labeling') because both masks ARE that one labeling's product. Option (a) Waymo requires a SECOND set of camera-only checkpoints with released per-voxel scores on Occ3D-Waymo — which runs straight into the same KILL-FEASIBILITY (no CUDA) wall the plan flags for H1, and the plan never confirms such checkpoints exist. So the leg that is supposed to earn external validity either uses a non-independent substrate (b) or an unbudgeted one (a).
  - fix: Demote (b) from 'second substrate / external validity' to what it is — a mask-robustness sensitivity check within one dataset — and stop claiming it pre-empts the labeling-specificity kill. Make the genuine generalization claim contingent ONLY on (a) Waymo with confirmed score-releasing checkpoints; if those do not exist, honestly scope the published claim to single-dataset (Occ3D-nuScenes) and label transfer as UNTESTED, rather than implying it was tested via (b).
- **major**: HASTY GENERALIZATION FROM n=2 CHECKPOINTS, with a self-sealing escape that removes falsifiability. The general claim is 'released camera-only occupancy models are systematically more over-confident in unobserved voxels.' The evidence is 2 checkpoints. Two is the minimum for 'not literally one model' but is far below what 'systematically, as a property of camera-only occupancy models' requires for a top venue — and the two are likely from the SAME architecture family (BEVFormer/FB-OCC lineage dominates Occ3D-nuScenes camera leaderboards), so they share inductive bias and a near-identical training recipe; agreement between them is weak evidence of generality. Worse, the kill criterion is self-sealing in the confirming direction: if both agree → 'systematic effect'; the only thing that kills is sign DISagreement. There is no provision for the case where both agree because they share the same confound (same loss-masking, same backbone) — agreement is treated as generality when it may be shared-cause.
  - fix: Require the 2 checkpoints to be from DIFFERENT architecture families (e.g. one BEVFormer-lineage, one fundamentally different head) and state the families in pre-registration; if only one family ships scores, cap the conclusion at 'observed on the BEVFormer-family checkpoints we could score' and drop 'camera-only occupancy models' as the subject. Add a third checkpoint if at all feasible — n=2 with shared lineage cannot support a 'systematic property of the model class' claim at a top venue.
- **minor**: UNFALSIFIABLE-BY-RESCOPE LADDER: every kill/pivot branch lands on a publishable result, which is good for the researcher but is an inductive red flag the plan does not acknowledge. KILL→'measured null is publishable'; PIVOT-1→'re-scope to far-field ECE'; PIVOT-2→'single-dataset note'; KILL-FEASIBILITY→'written protocol, blocked-on-compute.' There is NO outcome that the plan treats as 'this stage produced nothing.' A hypothesis whose every branch is a win is, at the meta level, not risking anything — and a reviewer will read the 'honest null is publishable' framing as pre-loading a face-saving exit rather than a genuine test. This doesn't make any single claim false, but it weakens the epistemic posture: the plan never says what would make it CONCLUDE THE WHOLE DIRECTION WAS NOT WORTH A PAPER.
  - fix: State one genuine non-publish condition explicitly (e.g. 'if the gap is null AND the only surviving rescope is the already-known far-field-is-harder effect, this is a negative note in an appendix, not a paper'). Distinguish 'publishable negative result' (null that contradicts a widely-held belief) from 'rescoped to a known effect' (not novel) — the plan currently treats both as wins.
- **minor**: 'MEASURES-not-JUDGES' FENCE IS ASSERTED BUT THE MOTIVATION SECTION CROSSES IT, a latent over-generalized conclusion. scope_out correctly forbids the danger/planning claim, yet motivation opens with 'A planner that trusts a high-confidence free in an unobserved voxel is trusting a learned prior, not a measurement' — which is exactly the planning-relevance/danger implication the fence disowns. The paper's framing thus smuggles back the safety-relevance the formal claim refuses, so a reviewer can attack the contribution as either over-claimed (if the danger framing is load-bearing for significance) or unmotivated (if it is not). You cannot use 'planners would be misled' to justify why the gap matters and simultaneously disclaim 'we do not assert the gap is dangerous.'
  - fix: Pick one: either keep the strict calibration-only claim and re-motivate significance WITHOUT the planner-misled narrative (e.g. 'calibration is reported as a single whole-grid number that is uninformative under heavy occlusion' — a methodological gap, not a danger claim), or own a modest, explicitly-bounded relevance claim and move it inside scope. Do not let motivation assert what scope_out forbids.
